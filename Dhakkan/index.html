Dhakkan

A pressure cooker without a lid is just a pot. The lid is what builds pressure, what transforms things faster, what makes the whistle blow. But a lid that is sealed too tight can explode.

You have been putting lids on things your whole life. On your frustration in class. On your tears in public. On the questions you are afraid to ask. Some of these lids are wise — not everything needs to boil over. But some have been on too long and the pressure is building.

Name the lids. Decide which ones to keep and which ones to gently lift before they blow.
