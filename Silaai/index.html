Silāī

the stitching

Silaai is the act of stitching — joining what was separate, mending what was torn. A needle enters, a thread follows, and two pieces of cloth become one. The stitch is the smallest unit of repair. Silāī asks you to name the things you have mended with patience — the relationships, beliefs, or parts of yourself that were torn and stitched back together, seam by seam.

Silāī

The Japanese have kintsugi — mending pottery with gold. Indians have silaai — mending cloth with thread. It is humbler. The thread matches the fabric. The stitch tries to be invisible. This is a different philosophy of repair: not celebrating the break, but quietly restoring wholeness. The garment is meant to be worn again, not displayed.

Your mending is like this. Quiet. Persistent. Stitch by stitch. The apology that took weeks to offer. The trust rebuilt one honest act at a time. The self-belief sewn back together with small daily proofs.

Name the stitchings. They are the quiet labour of making things whole again.
