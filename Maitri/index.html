Maitrī

loving-kindness

In every tradition, there is a practice of expanding the circle of care — starting with yourself and reaching outward.

Five rings. Five people. For each one, name them and send a kind thought.

Start with yourself. End with the hardest person to love. Watch the circles glow.

All five circles are glowing.

You sent kindness to yourself, to someone you love, to a friend, to someone you barely know, and to someone who is difficult. That is the full circle of compassion.

The kindness you send out does not leave you. It multiplies.

Maitrī मैत्री

Maitrī (Pali: mettā) means loving-kindness — the unconditional wish for the well-being of all beings. In Buddhist practice, it begins with the self and expands outward in concentric circles.

The five circles are traditional: yourself, a loved one, a friend, a neutral person, and a difficult person. The practice is not about forcing feelings. It is about intending kindness, even when it is hard.

Everything stays on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere.
