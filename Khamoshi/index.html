the silence

Not all silences are the same. There is the silence of a car ride after a fight. The silence of sitting with a friend and needing no words. The silence of a house after someone leaves. The silence you choose and the silence forced upon you. Khamoshi asks you to name the silences in your life — and listen to what each one is saying.

Khāmoshī

Silence is never empty. It is always full of something — anger, love, grief, peace, or fear.

The silence between you and your father on the drive home may hold more words than a whole conversation. The silence of an empty room after a loved one leaves is deafening. The silence of two friends who understand each other is the loudest form of connection.

Learn the language of silence. It is spoken everywhere but taught nowhere. Name the silences. They have been talking to you all along.
