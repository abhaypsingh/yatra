Zanjeirī

The duality of the chain is the duality of every human connection. The same link that holds a prisoner holds an anchor. The same bond that confines also stabilises. You cannot always tell whether a chain is keeping you captive or keeping you safe — sometimes you only know years later, when you are free and drifting, or when you are held and grateful.

You have worn chains. Relationships that felt like prisons but were actually moorings. Obligations that felt like anchors but were actually cages. The metal was the same. The difference was in what you understood only later.

Name the chains. They are the links that defined whether you were bound or held.
