the plate

A thali is not just food on a plate. It is a mother’s love portioned into katoris. It is a festival condensed into six flavours. It is the belief that a complete meal, like a complete life, requires sweet and bitter and sour and spicy. Thaali asks you to name the plates that fed you — the meals that were more than meals.

Thālī

In India, we do not just eat. We share a thali. Everything is there at once — the dal, the roti, the sabzi, the pickle, the sweet. Life on a plate. You eat with your hands because food is not just fuel, it is connection.

The thali your mother makes when you are sick is different from the one she makes for a guest. The thali at a wedding is different from the one at a funeral. Each plate tells a story. Each katori holds a feeling.

Name the plates. The ones that nourished your body and the ones that fed your soul.
