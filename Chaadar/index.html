Chādar

the bedsheet

There is a saying: jitni chaadar ho utne pair phelao — stretch your legs only as far as the sheet allows. The chaadar defines your limits. It covers, it comforts, it constrains. Chaadar asks you to name the sheets of your life — the limits you were given and whether you stayed within them.

Chādar

The chaadar is honest. It does not pretend to be bigger than it is. It teaches you the relationship between desire and reality. You can want a king-sized life, but if your sheet is single-bed, your feet will be cold.

But sometimes the chaadar is wrong. Sometimes you need to get a bigger sheet. Sometimes the limits placed on you were someone else’s fear, not your reality. The wisdom is in knowing which chaadars to respect and which to outgrow.

Name the sheets. The ones that kept you warm and the ones that kept you small.
