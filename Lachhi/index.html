Lachhī

Everyone wants the butter. The prize, the goal, the thing you churned for. But the lachhi — the thin, sour liquid left behind — is what actually cools you on a hot afternoon, settles a troubled stomach, restores what the effort took. The residue is often more valuable than the intended product. The by-product is often the real gift.

You have lachhi in your life. The friendships formed while pursuing a career that didn’t work out. The lessons learned from a project that failed. The person you became while chasing a goal you never reached. The churning produced something you didn’t expect.

Name the by-products. They are the nourishment that the churning left behind.
