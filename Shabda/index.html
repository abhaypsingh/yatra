Shabda

the weight of words

Words are not equal. The same sentence can be a feather or a stone, depending on who says it, when, and what they are carrying.

“I’m fine.” From a stranger, it means nothing. From your father after you cancel dinner, it is a door closing.

You will see ordinary sentences placed in different lives. The words stay the same. Everything else changes. This is about learning to feel the weight before you speak — and after you listen.

Every word you say lands somewhere. Know where it lands before you let it go.

Words remember

Every sentence you have ever said is still living somewhere — in someone’s memory, shaping how they see you, shaping how they see themselves.

You cannot take words back. But you can learn to feel their weight before you let them go. That is not silence. That is care.

Shabda शब्द

Shabda (Sanskrit: word, sound, testimony) is one of the valid means of knowledge in Indian philosophy. Words are not mere sounds — they carry truth, intention, and consequence. In the Vedic tradition, the universe itself was spoken into being.

This experience is about the invisible weight that sentences carry. The same words become different things in different mouths, at different moments, between different people. Learning to feel that weight is the beginning of speaking with care.

No words you read here are recorded or stored. They pass through, as all words do.
