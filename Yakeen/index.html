Yakeen is belief — not the belief that comes from evidence but the belief that arrives despite its absence. It is trust before proof. Yaqīn asks you to name the beliefs that held you when evidence alone could not.

Yaqīn

Faith is a structural element. It is the invisible beam that holds up the visible house. You cannot see it, you cannot prove it, but without it the whole structure collapses. Yakeen is not naivety — it is the deliberate choice to trust when the evidence is incomplete, because some things can only be built on foundations that cannot be measured.

You have relied on yakeen. The belief that things would work out when nothing suggested they would. The trust in a person before they had earned it. The faith in yourself when your track record said otherwise. These beliefs were not rational. They were structural.
