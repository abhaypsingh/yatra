Siyāhī

Before ink there was speech, and speech fades. But ink stays. And in your generation, almost everything is ink — texts, posts, screenshots, voice notes forwarded without consent.

You have already written things you cannot take back. Maybe a cruel reply. Maybe a truth said at the wrong time. Maybe a photograph shared that should have stayed private.

This is not about guilt. It is about awareness. Every word you put into the world is ink. It dries fast. Name what you have written. Let the permanence make you more careful, not more silent.
