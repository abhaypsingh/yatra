Āghāz

Before anything becomes a story, it has an aaghaz. Not the kind you plan for months — the kind that happens in a single breath. You pick up the phone and dial. You open the door and walk out. You say the word you’ve been holding. That instant, trembling and irreversible, is the aaghaz. Everything before it was rehearsal.

Most people think beginnings are hopeful. They can be terrifying. To begin is to admit that what came before was not enough, that you need something new, that you are willing to be a beginner again. The aaghaz carries both courage and vulnerability in equal measure.

Record your beginnings. Not just the triumphant ones, but the quiet, frightened, necessary ones that no one else witnessed.
