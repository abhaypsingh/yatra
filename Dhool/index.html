Dust is what remains when movement stops. After a storm, after construction, after demolition — the dust settles and you see the new shape of things.

Your life has these moments too. The week after exam results, when everything looks the same but nothing feels like it did. The month after a friend moved away, when their absence has become ordinary.

Dust is not dramatic. It is quiet. It is the part of change that nobody talks about — the slow return to normal when normal is no longer what it was. Name the dust. It is telling you what has truly changed.
