Others poisoned the well for years. Takrār

Every family has its arguments that are remembered like historical events — dated, located, recounted differently by each witness. The fight at the dining table in 1998. The phone call that ended in hanging up. The door that slammed so hard the frame cracked. Arguments are the earthquakes of relationships, and their aftershocks can last for decades.

But not all arguments destroy. Some are the only way truth gets spoken in families and friendships where politeness has become a prison. The daughter who finally told her father he was wrong. The friend who said what everyone was thinking. The couple who fought through to the other side and found, in the wreckage, a honesty they had never reached before.

Name the arguments that shaped your relationships. Each one, whether it healed or harmed, revealed what you and the other person truly needed to say.
