A pagdandi is not a road. It is a trail worn into existence by the feet of those who had no road. It is the path through the fields, the shortcut through the trees, the way you find when no one has built one for you. Pagdandi asks you to name the trails — the paths you made when there was no path.

Pagḍanḍī

Roads are built by governments. Pagdandis are built by people who need to get somewhere. They are democratic, organic, honest. They go where the road does not bother to go.

You have walked pagdandis your whole life. The way you figured out maths that no one taught you properly. The friendship you built when you were the new kid. The career you are inventing because no one in your family has done it before.

Name the trails. They are proof that you do not need a paved road to reach where you are going.
