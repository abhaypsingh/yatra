Surāhī

The surahi is designed for restraint. Its narrow neck means you cannot gulp. You must tilt, wait, receive. This is not a flaw — it is philosophy. Some things should not be consumed quickly.

Your father’s wisdom came like surahi water. Not in lectures but in small, cool sips over years. A grandmother’s stories. A teacher’s patience. Love that does not announce itself but simply keeps you cool when life is burning.

Name the surahis. The slow pourers. The patient givers. The ones whose narrow necks hid an ocean of kindness.
