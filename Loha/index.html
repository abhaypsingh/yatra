Iron can become a bridge or a prison bar. A plough or a sword. What makes you strong can also make you rigid, unbending, unable to adapt. Loha asks you to name the iron in you — the stubbornness that saves you and the stubbornness that traps you. What has been forged in you, and is it the right shape?

Lohā

We praise iron. We say “loha manna” — to accept someone’s iron, to acknowledge their strength. But iron unchecked becomes a cage.

The determination that got you through a hard time can become rigidity that prevents you from accepting help. The independence you are proud of can become loneliness you refuse to admit. The toughness that protected you as a child can make you unable to be soft as an adult.

Name your iron. Decide what shape you want it to take. A bridge is iron too.
