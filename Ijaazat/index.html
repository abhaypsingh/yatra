Ijāzat

the permission

Ijaazat is permission — the moment someone says “you may” and a door that was closed swings open. Some permissions are granted by authority; others by love; others by your own self finally allowing what you always wanted. Ijāzat asks you to name the permissions that set something free in you.

Ijāzat

A single “yes” can unlock years of waiting. A father says: go, study what you love. A teacher says: you are ready. You say to yourself, at last: I am allowed to want this. The permission itself takes a moment, but it releases something that had been building pressure for years.

Not all permissions come from others. The hardest ijaazat is often the one you grant yourself — permission to fail, permission to change, permission to stop performing someone else’s version of your life.

Name the permissions. Each one marks a door that opened and a self that walked through it.
