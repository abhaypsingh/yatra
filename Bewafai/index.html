Bewafāī

Betrayal requires trust. A stranger cannot betray you — only someone you believed in can. This is what makes bewafai so devastating: it is not just the loss of a person or a promise, but the loss of your own judgement. You trusted, and you were wrong. The pain is not only in what they did, but in what it reveals about the gap between what you believed and what was real.

But bewafai also teaches. It teaches you where you gave trust too freely and where your instincts were shouting warnings you refused to hear. It teaches you that survival is possible after the unthinkable. And it teaches you, eventually, that the capacity to trust again — even after betrayal — is not naivety. It is courage.

Name what was broken. Not to dwell in bitterness, but to recognize the strength it took to continue trusting at all.
