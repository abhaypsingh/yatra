Qissā

External narratives shape identity more than we admit. A mother says “you were always the sensitive one” and the child becomes sensitive — not because it was true at first, but because the story was told so often that it became the mould. We grow into the shapes that others narrate for us, sometimes gratefully, sometimes resentfully, but always powerfully.

You have qisse. The story your father told about the time you were brave. The story your sibling told about the time you were impossible. The story a teacher told that made you believe you were capable. Each one carved a groove in your identity that your own actions then followed.

Name the stories. They are the narratives that built you from the outside in.
