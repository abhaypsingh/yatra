Ojhal

Some people are more vivid after they leave. Presence in absence is the strangest form of company — the person who is not there occupies more space in your mind than the people who are. Ojhal is not loss. It is a different kind of presence — one that does not require a body, a voice, or a reply. It requires only memory and the refusal to forget.

You have ojhal people. The childhood friend you lost track of. The teacher who moved away. The relative who stopped coming to gatherings. They are gone from your days but not from your interior world, where they continue to speak, to gesture, to matter.

Name them. Not to bring them back, but to honour their continued presence in your absence.
