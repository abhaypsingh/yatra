Ghaṇṭī

A bell does not ask you to listen. It simply rings, and you either hear it or you do not.

The bells in your life are the moments of sudden clarity — when something someone said made the world rearrange itself. When a line in a film made you cry and you did not know why. When you realised, mid-sentence, that you were wrong.

These moments are rare and precious. Write them down. Because bells fade, but their echo can guide you if you remember the sound.
