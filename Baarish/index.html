In India, rain is not weather — it is an event. The entire country waits for it. Farmers scan the sky. Children stand on rooftops. When it finally arrives, people do not run indoors — they run outside. The first baarish is celebrated because it is proof that the world has not forgotten you, that the cycle continues, that thirst is temporary.

Your life has had its droughts — stretches where nothing grew, where the ground inside you cracked and hardened. And then something came. A person, an opportunity, a word spoken at the right moment. It did not arrive gently. Relief rarely does. It poured.
