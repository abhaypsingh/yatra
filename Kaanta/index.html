Kānṭā

A kaanta in the foot stops you in your tracks. Everything pauses while you pull it out. That is what thorns do — they make you stop, pay attention, be present. Pain is a form of attention.

The thorns in your life are not random. The jealousy that comes with love. The loneliness that comes with independence. The criticism that comes with creating. Every beautiful thing you reach for has something sharp attached.

Name the thorns. They are not the enemy. They are the price of reaching for roses.
