A haandi cooks slowly. The lid stays on. The heat is low. Hours pass. And what emerges is something that quick cooking can never produce — depth, richness, flavour that has had time to become itself. Haandi asks you to name the slow-cooked things in your life — the relationships, skills, and understandings that needed time and patience to become what they are.

We live in the age of instant everything. Instant noodles, instant messages, instant gratification. But the best things in life are haandi things. They cannot be microwaved.

Trust takes years to build. Mastery takes a decade. Understanding your parents takes growing up. Forgiving someone takes as long as it takes. These are haandi processes — slow, covered, simmering, transforming.

Name the things that are still cooking. The ones that have already emerged rich and deep. Honour the time they needed. Not everything worthwhile is instant.
