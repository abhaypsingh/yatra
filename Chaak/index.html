A chaak spins, and the potter’s hands shape the spinning clay into form. Too much pressure and the pot collapses. Too little and it stays a shapeless lump. Chāk

The chaak is ancient and simple: a spinning disc, a lump of clay, two hands. But from this simplicity comes infinite form. The same wheel can produce a water pot, a lamp, a cup, a vase. The wheel does not decide the form — the hands do. And the hands must be both firm and gentle, knowing when to press and when to let the spin do the work.

Your life has been a chaak. The circumstances spun, and someone’s hands — a parent’s, a teacher’s, your own — applied just enough pressure to give you shape. You are the vessel that emerged from that balance of force and tenderness.

Name the wheels. They are where you were shaped from clay into form.
