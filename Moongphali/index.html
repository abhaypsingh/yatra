The peanut is nobody’s luxury. It is the snack of the common bench, the train platform, the winter evening. It costs almost nothing. It requires no plate, no fork, no kitchen. Just hands and company. And yet, some of the best conversations in Indian life happen over moongphali — because when the food is humble, the connection becomes the main course.

Your moongphali moments are the times when simplicity was the entire point. When you didn’t need a restaurant to feel nourished. When the company was so good that the simplest food tasted like a feast.
