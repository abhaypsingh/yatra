Ibārat

Most words pass through us like water. We hear thousands every day and retain almost none. But occasionally, an expression arrives that lodges itself permanently — a sentence from a book, a line from a conversation, a phrase overheard at a funeral. The ibaarat is language that transcends communication and becomes something closer to revelation.

These expressions survive because they said something you already knew but could not articulate. They gave shape to the shapeless. A master calligrapher does not invent the alphabet — they reveal what was always possible within the letters. An ibaarat does the same with meaning.

Collect the expressions that changed how you see. They are a library of your deepest recognitions.
