the knot

Some things inside you are tangled. A fight you never resolved. A compliment you did not believe. A guilt you carry but cannot name. These are your knots — emotions, memories, and thoughts twisted together until you cannot tell which thread is which. Girah asks you to name them. Not to fix them. Just to find them.

A knot forms when two things get twisted together. Anger and love. Pride and shame. The desire to be close and the fear of being hurt.

You cannot untie a knot you cannot see. And some knots are not meant to be undone — they are what holds things together.

But the knots that hurt are the ones you ignore. Name them. Trace the threads. Sometimes just naming a tangle is the first step to loosening it.
