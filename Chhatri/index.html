A chhatri is portable shelter — you carry it folded, and open it only when the storm comes. It does not stop the rain. It simply keeps you dry enough to keep walking. Chhatrī

The genius of the umbrella is not that it stops rain. It is that it is portable. You do not build a roof — you carry one. And when the sky clears, you fold it back and walk lighter. The umbrella is optimism disguised as preparation: you acknowledge the storm will come, and you prepare to walk through it anyway.

Your chhatris are the things you carry just in case. The emergency fund. The phone number of someone wise. The breathing technique. The prayer. They weigh almost nothing when folded, but they are everything when opened.

Name the umbrellas. They are how you walk through rain without stopping.
