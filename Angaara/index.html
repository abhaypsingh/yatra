Angārā

An ember is what remains after the fire dies down. It looks like nothing — grey ash, a faint warmth. But blow on it gently and it will light again. Your passions are the same.

The world will try to put your embers out. People will tell you to be practical, to move on, to grow up. But the things that glow inside you — the curiosity, the care, the stubborn fascination — those are not childish. They are the truest parts of you.

Name them. Tend them. An ember only dies when it is forgotten.
