the cage

Some cages have visible bars — rules, expectations, circumstances. But the most powerful cages are invisible. The belief that you are not smart enough. The need to please everyone. The fear of what people will say. Pinjra asks you to name the cages you live in — the ones others built and the ones you built yourself.

What keeps you contained?

Not every cage is bad. The cage of discipline keeps you focused. The cage of routine keeps you sane. Some cages are training grounds, not prisons.

But the cages that shrink you — the ones that make you smaller than you are — those deserve to be named. Because you cannot leave a cage you refuse to see.

Ask yourself: is this cage protecting me, or is it shrinking me? The answer will tell you whether to stay or step out.
