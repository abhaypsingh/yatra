Mohallā

In a mohalla, your mother is not the only one who can scold you. Any aunty can. And any uncle can give you a sweet. Privacy is limited but so is loneliness. Everyone knows your business, but everyone also shows up when you need help.

The mohalla is disappearing. Apartments with closed doors. Neighbours who nod but never speak. Deliveries instead of visits. What is gained in privacy is lost in connection. The mohalla was not just a place. It was a way of being human together.

Name the neighbourhoods. The ones that held you. The ones you miss. The ones you are trying to rebuild.
