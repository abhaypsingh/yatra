Kashtī

the boat

A kashti carries you across water you cannot cross alone — a river of grief, a sea of uncertainty, a flood of change. The boat does not remove the water; it simply keeps you above it long enough to reach the other side. Kashtī asks you to name the vessels — people, beliefs, circumstances — that carried you across.

Kashtī

The kashti appears in Urdu poetry as the fragile thing between you and the ocean — a few planks of wood between you and drowning. It is not grand. It leaks. It rocks. But it floats. And sometimes floating is all you need until the shore appears.

Your boats have taken many forms. A person who listened when you needed to speak. A routine that gave you structure when everything else was chaos. A belief that kept you moving when logic said to stop. None of them were perfect vessels. But they carried you.

Name the boats. Honour what kept you above the water.
