A lehar rises from depth, travels a great distance, touches the shore, and returns. It does not stay. It cannot be held. Yet it shapes the coastline over lifetimes. Lehar asks you to name the waves in your life — the forces that arrived from somewhere deep, touched you powerfully, and then receded, leaving you permanently reshaped.

Lehar

A wave is not water moving forward — it is energy passing through water. The water stays roughly where it is. The wave travels. This means that what touched you was not the thing itself but the force moving through it. The person who changed your life may not have intended to. The event that reshaped you may have been incidental. But the wave was real.

Your lehars are the surges of emotion, insight, or change that rose, crested, and fell back. They did not stay — but your coastline is different because they came.

Name the waves. They are the invisible forces that shaped your visible shore.
