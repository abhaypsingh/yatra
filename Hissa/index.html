Every inheritance, every meal, every family is divided into hissas — portions that define who gets what and how much. Some shares are fair, some are generous, some are cruelties disguised as tradition. Hissā asks you to name the shares you claimed, received, or surrendered — and what each portion taught you about belonging and worth.

Hissā

In joint families, the hissa is everything. Land divided among sons. Jewellery distributed among daughters. Responsibilities portioned out — who takes care of the aging parent, who pays for the wedding, who gets the front room. The hissa is never just about property. It is about love measured, worth assigned, belonging confirmed or denied.

You have received your shares. Some were handed to you with warmth. Some you had to fight for. Some you gave away because keeping them cost too much. And some shares you never received — attention, affection, credit — though they were rightfully yours.
