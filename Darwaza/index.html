Darwāzā

the door

A darwaza is both invitation and barrier — it depends which side you stand on. Every door is a threshold between one life and another, between the known and the unknown, between staying and leaving. To open a door is an act of faith; to close one is an act of finality. Darwāzā asks you to name the doors that opened or closed and changed everything.

Darwāzā

In old Indian homes, the darwaza is never just functional. It is carved, painted, adorned with marigolds and mango leaves during festivals. It is where shoes are removed and identities shift — the person you are outside becomes the person you are inside. The threshold is sacred because it marks the boundary between worlds.

Your life is a corridor of doors. Some you opened yourself — with courage, with naivety, with desperation. Some were opened for you by people who saw something you couldn’t. And some were shut in your face, and that closing became the most important thing that ever happened to you, because it sent you somewhere else.

Name the doors. Honour the thresholds that made you cross into who you are.
