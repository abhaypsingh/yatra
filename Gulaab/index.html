Gulāb

The gulaab holds a central place in South Asian culture — in gardens, in poetry, in the water sprinkled at gatherings. Gulaab jal, rose water, is distilled beauty. But the rose bush itself is unforgiving. Anyone who has tended one knows: you bleed for the blooms. The plant does not apologise for its thorns. They are not a flaw — they are the price of the flower.

You have reached for roses. A relationship that was magnificent and painful in equal measure. An ambition that demanded everything. A truth that was beautiful to know and terrible to carry. The thorns did not stop you. Perhaps they should not have.

Name the roses. Honour both the bloom and the blood.
