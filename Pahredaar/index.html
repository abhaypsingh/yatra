Pahredār

The watchman’s gift is wakefulness. While you sleep, he walks. While you dream, he listens. His vigilance is an act of love so quiet that you only notice it in its absence — when one night the whistle doesn’t sound and the silence feels unsafe.

Every life has its pahredaars. The parent who stays up until you come home. The friend who checks in when you go quiet. The mentor who sees the danger you are walking toward and says something, even when it is unwelcome. They do not fight your battles. They simply watch, and their watching keeps the darkness at bay.

Name your watchmen. Their sleeplessness was the price of your peace.
