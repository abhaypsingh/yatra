Ābrū

the honour

Aabroo is honour — not reputation, which is what others think, but honour, which is what you know about yourself. Aabroo is the inner ledger. You can fool others about your aabroo but not yourself. Ābrū asks you to name the moments when your honour was tested and what you chose to protect.

Ābrū

Reputation is what you show the world. Aabroo is what you know when the door is closed and you are alone with yourself. It is the inner ledger — the account you keep of the times you stood firm and the times you buckled. Others may never know the difference, but you always will.

Aabroo is tested in the quiet moments, not the grand ones. The small lie that would make things easier. The credit taken for someone else’s work. The silence kept when speaking up would cost you something. Each test leaves a mark on the inner ledger, and no amount of outer praise can erase an entry you know is there.

Name the tests. They are the moral accounting of your life, visible only to you.
