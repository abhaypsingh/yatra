Sangam

the confluence

In India, the place where two rivers meet is called a sangam. The waters do not fight. They do not ask which river is right. They simply flow into one.

The hardest thing about growing up — especially when a family breaks apart — is learning that two opposite things can both be true at the same time.

You can love someone and be angry at them. You can miss someone and not want to see them. You can need someone and push them away.

This is not confusion. This is being human. Let both rivers flow.

You do not have to choose between what you feel and what is true. You do not have to pick a side within yourself. The place where contradictions meet is not weakness. It is depth.

Every person you love is both wonderful and flawed. Every feeling you have is both valid and incomplete. The sangam — the meeting — is where wisdom begins.

Sangam संगम

Sangam (Sanskrit: confluence, meeting place) is the sacred point where two rivers merge into one. The most famous is the Triveni Sangam at Prayagraj, where the Ganga, Yamuna, and the invisible Sarasvati meet. Pilgrims travel there to witness the union of separate waters.

This experience is about a different confluence — the place inside you where contradictory truths meet. Not to resolve them, but to let them coexist. Growth begins when you stop trying to choose between feelings that are both real.

There are no wrong answers here. There are only rivers, flowing as they must.
