Sabaq

In the old tradition, a sabaq was the daily portion of the Quran a student would memorise. But the word has grown beyond the madrasa. It now means any lesson life presses into you — the kind you cannot forget because it was written not on paper but on experience. A burnt hand teaches more about fire than any warning ever could.

Your sabaq came at unexpected moments. The friendship that ended and taught you what loyalty actually costs. The job you lost and the self-worth you found underneath the title. The illness that reorganised every priority you thought was fixed. These lessons did not ask permission. They arrived, and you were changed.

Name the lessons. They are the curriculum no one designed but everyone receives.
