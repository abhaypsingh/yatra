Girvī

Girvi is a temporary sacrifice — you hand over something precious because you believe the future will let you reclaim it. A mother pawns her jewellery so her son can take an exam in another city. A farmer pledges his land against a season’s seed. The pawn shop holds your treasure while you go out to earn the right to take it back.

But not everything pawned is redeemed. Some pledges are never reclaimed. The jewellery stays in the shop. The land changes hands. And you learn that the future you trusted was not the future that arrived.

Name what you pawned. Whether you got it back or not, the act of pledging tells you what you believed was coming.
