A pahiya turns. That is all it does. But in that turning, it carries the weight of the entire cart forward. It meets the same ground again and again, yet each revolution covers new distance. Pahiyā

The wheel is the oldest metaphor for progress hidden inside repetition. Every point on the rim passes through the same position again and again. If you watch a single spot on the wheel, it seems to go nowhere. But if you watch the cart, it has crossed a continent. The spot was never going in circles — it was drawing a cycloid, moving forward with every turn.

Your daily routines are pahiyas. The same morning, the same commute, the same bedtime ritual. It feels repetitive. But the cart — your life — has moved enormous distances because of those turns.

Name the wheels. They are the faithful cycles that carried everything forward.
