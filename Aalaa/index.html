the shelf niche

An aalaa is a small niche built into a wall — a shelf that is part of the house itself. Before cupboards, every home had aalas. They held the diya, the matchbox, the photograph of a grandparent. Small spaces for small, sacred things. Aalaa asks you to name the niches — the small, built-in spaces in your life where you keep what matters most.

Ālā

The aalaa is architecture’s acknowledgment that we need places for precious things. Not big places — just a niche. A recess in the wall. Enough for a diya, a photo, a small idol. The aalaa says: this wall is not just structure. It has space carved into it for what matters.

Your personality has aalas too. Small, built-in spaces where you keep your core beliefs. Your non-negotiables. The memories you visit every day. The values so embedded they are part of your walls, not just placed on shelves.

Name the niches. They are the architecture of your inner house.
