Every life is a map of paths. Some you planned — the sport you picked, the friend you approached. Some were laid out before you arrived — the city you were born in, the language you first spoke, the religion of your family.

Looking back, it can be hard to tell which paths were chosen and which were given. And perhaps it does not matter. What matters is what you did with each one — how you walked it, what you noticed, who you became along the way.

Trace your paths. The planned and the accidental. They all led here.
