the ring

A ring has no beginning and no end. Some circles in your life are like that — cycles you repeat, promises that loop back, habits that go round and round. Some rings are chosen with love. Some trap you in repetition. Angoothi asks you to name the circles in your life — the ones that hold you and the ones that spin you.

Some circles are sacred — the cycle of seasons, the rhythm of a family gathering every Sunday, the annual phone call to a distant uncle. These rings connect you to something larger than yourself.

Other circles are traps — the same argument with the same person ending the same way. The cycle of procrastination and guilt. The pattern of making a promise and breaking it.

A ring only becomes a cage when you do not know you are inside it. Name the circles. Once seen, you can choose which ones to keep wearing and which to take off.
