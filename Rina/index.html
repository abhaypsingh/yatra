Rina

In Sanskrit, rina means a debt — but not the kind you repay with money. It is the debt of love, of kindness given freely, of care that asks nothing in return.

This is a ledger for the invisible things people do for you. Dad making chai before you wake. A friend who texts just to check in. A stranger holding a door. A teacher who believed in you.

You cannot repay these debts. But you can notice them.
