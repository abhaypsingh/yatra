Dhabbā

the stain

A dhabba is a stain that will not wash out. Ink on a school shirt. Turmeric on white cloth. A mistake on an otherwise clean record. Dhabba asks you to name the stains — the marks that stayed no matter how hard you tried to erase them, and whether they ruined the cloth or gave it character.

Dhabbā

We are taught that stains are bad. That clean is good. That a spotless record, a spotless reputation, a spotless shirt are what to strive for. But have you ever seen cloth that has been lived in? It has stains. And those stains are its history.

The dhabba of haldi from a wedding. The ink stain from the exam that changed your life. The grease mark from the first time you fixed something with your hands. Not all stains are shame. Some are evidence of a life fully lived.

Name the stains. Decide which are blemishes and which are badges.
