Gokhrā

the ventilation grate

A gokhra is a decorative ventilation opening in old Indian walls — carved stone or cement that lets air pass through without letting the eye see clearly. It is privacy and breath at once. Light enters in patterns. Air circulates. But the wall remains a wall. Gokhrā asks you to name the small openings in your boundaries that let you breathe — the places where you are closed but not sealed.

Gokhrā

The gokhra is brilliant architecture: a wall that breathes. It understands that being enclosed does not mean being sealed. That privacy does not require suffocation. That a boundary can have porosity without losing its purpose. The gokhra filters — it lets air through in delicate patterns while keeping the world at the right distance.

You need gokhras. The emotional walls you build need ventilation. The professional boundaries you maintain need small openings for honesty. Without gokhras, your walls become prisons. With them, they become homes.
