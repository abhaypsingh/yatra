Gaddī

the cushion

A gaddi is both a cushion and a throne. It is where you sit when you are comfortable and where a king sits when he rules. The same word for softness and for power. Gaddi asks you to name the seats you have occupied — the comfortable ones, the powerful ones, and the ones you were never allowed to sit on.

Gaddī

In every family, there is an invisible seating arrangement. The eldest gets the best seat. The guest gets the cushion. The child sits on the floor. Where you sit tells you where you stand.

A gaddi can be a place of rest or a place of power. The same cushion that comforts a tired body also elevates the person who sits on it. Growing up is learning which seats are yours to claim and which thrones are not worth the weight of their crowns.

Name the seats. The ones that held you. The ones that excluded you. The ones you are still reaching for.
