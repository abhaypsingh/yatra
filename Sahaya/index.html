Sahāya सहाय

Sahāya means helper, companion, one who assists. In Sanskrit, the word carries the sense of walking alongside — not carrying someone, but joining them on the path.

Research consistently shows that the willingness to ask for help is a sign of emotional intelligence, not weakness. People who can identify when they need support and reach for it recover faster from setbacks and build stronger relationships.

The hardest part is not finding help. It is admitting you need it.

Everything stays on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere.
