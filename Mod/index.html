A turn is not always dramatic. Sometimes it is a sentence someone says at dinner that rearranges your whole understanding of the world. Sometimes it is a door that closes and forces you down a hallway you never planned to walk.

What matters is not whether the turn was big or small. What matters is that after it, you were different. You cannot unlearn what you learned. You cannot unsee what you saw.

Write down the moments that bent your path. They are the geography of who you are.
