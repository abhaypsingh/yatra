Chaunk is the sizzle of mustard seeds and cumin hitting hot oil. It is the final act of cooking dal — a small addition that transforms everything. Without chaunk, the dal is bland. With it, the whole pot comes alive. Chaunk asks you to name the small additions that transformed everything — the tiny interventions that changed the flavour of your whole life.

The chaunk happens in seconds. Hot oil. A few seeds. A dried chilli. The sizzle. Pour it over the dal. Done. But those seconds change everything. The dal goes from nutritious-but-boring to home-cooked-with-love.

Life has chaunk moments too. A teacher who said one sentence that changed your direction. A book you picked up randomly that rewired your thinking. A stranger’s kindness on a terrible day. Small. Fast. Transformative.
