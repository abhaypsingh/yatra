Gaunā

the departure

Gauna is the ceremony when a bride leaves her parents’ home for the last time — the threshold she crosses that divides before and after. The whole family weeps. The doli is lifted. The daughter looks back, and then forward. Gaunā asks you to name the departures in your life that divided time into before and after — the thresholds you crossed knowing you could not return as you were.

Gaunā

The gauna is the most honest ritual in Indian life. It does not pretend that leaving is painless. The tears are expected. The grief is public. Everyone acknowledges: something is ending so something else can begin. The bride does not sneak away — she leaves in full ceremony, with the weight of the moment honoured.

Your gaunas may not have had ceremony, but they had the same weight. The last day at a job. The last night in a childhood home. The moment you became responsible for another life. Each one crossed a line you could not uncross.

Name the departures. They are where your life turned its most important corners.
