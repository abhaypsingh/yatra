Saugāt

A saugaat is evidence of remembering. Someone was far away — in another city, another country, another chapter of their life — and they saw something and thought of you. That is the real gift: not the object but the proof of distance crossed by thought. The trinket from a trip, the sweet from a hometown, the fabric from a market — each one says: I was there, and you were here, and I carried you with me.

You have received saugaat. Small things that meant everything because of the distance they travelled and the love they carried. Objects whose worth was not in rupees but in the fact that someone, somewhere far away, was thinking of you.
