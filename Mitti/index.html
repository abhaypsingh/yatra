Miṭṭī

Before you were you, there was soil — the place, the people, the rituals that formed you. You did not choose your mother tongue, your childhood meals, or the faith you were raised in. But they live in you like minerals in the earth.

Some things you carry with pride. Some you question. Some you only notice when you leave home and discover that not everyone speaks, eats, or prays the way you do.

Miṭṭī asks you to notice the ground you stand on. Not to judge it — just to know it.
