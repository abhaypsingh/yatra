Haathkadhī

There is a paradox at the heart of every chosen bond: the hand willingly shackled is the hand that builds. The person who says “I will stay” gives up a thousand possible departures, and in that surrender finds a freedom that the uncommitted never know. The shackle is not the opposite of liberty — it is the price of a deeper kind.

You have worn shackles too. A promise to a person. A duty to a calling. A vow to a place or a principle. Each one closed around your wrist and each one, paradoxically, opened a door that could not have been opened by a free hand.

Name the shackles. They are the bindings that made you whole.
