Goṭhrī

the bundle

A goṭhrī is a cloth bundle — the kind tied at four corners and carried on the head or shoulder when moving from one place to another. It holds everything you chose to take and nothing you chose to leave behind. The goṭhrī is edited life: not everything you own, but everything you decided was worth carrying. Goṭhrī asks you to name the bundles you have tied — the curated collections of things, memories, values, and habits that you carried from one chapter of life to the next.

Goṭhrī

The goṭhrī is the original suitcase — a cloth spread flat, possessions placed in the centre, corners gathered and knotted. It carries no more than what one person can lift. This limitation is its wisdom: it forces you to choose. You cannot bring everything. You must decide what is essential and what is merely familiar.

Your bundles were the curated packages of identity you carried through transitions. Leaving home: what did you pack? Leaving a relationship: what did you take with you? Leaving a career: which skills, which lessons, which scars did you tie into the cloth and hoist onto your shoulder? The bundle reveals your values better than any statement of purpose.

Name the bundles. What you chose to carry tells the truest story of who you are.
