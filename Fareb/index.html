Fareb

The cruelest part of deception is not the lie itself. It is the realisation that your own perception failed you — that you looked at something false and saw it as true, that you trusted your judgement and your judgement was wrong. Fareb does not just damage the relationship with the deceiver; it damages the relationship with yourself. You begin to doubt your ability to read the world.

But in the wreckage of every unmasked deception lies a harder, more useful kind of sight. You learn to notice what you previously ignored — the inconsistency that should have alarmed you, the explanation that was too smooth, the generosity that was strategic rather than genuine. The scar tissue of fareb becomes a new organ of perception.
