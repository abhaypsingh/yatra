Hunnar

Hunnar is embodied knowledge — the wisdom that bypasses the mind entirely and lives in the muscles, the fingertips, the wrists. A weaver’s hands move the shuttle without thought. A cook salts the pot without measuring. The body remembers what the mind has long stopped paying attention to.

This is muscle memory at its deepest: not just a skill, but an identity. You are what your hands know how to do. The carpenter is not a person who happens to cut wood — he is a person whose hands think in wood.

Name the crafts your hands carry. They are the knowledge that cannot be unlearned.
