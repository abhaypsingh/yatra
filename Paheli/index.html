Pahelī

Children in India grow up with pahelis — riddles told by grandparents on verandas, puzzles that delight because the answer is always hiding in plain sight. But as you grow older, the riddles become less playful. They become the questions that sit at the centre of your life and refuse to move. Why did they leave? Was it my fault? What would have happened if I had said yes instead of no?

The wisdom is not in solving these riddles but in learning to carry them. Some questions are not meant to be answered. They are meant to be lived with — turned over in the hand like a smooth stone, examined from every angle, and eventually accepted as part of the landscape of being human.

Name the riddles. Not to solve them, but to honour the weight of not knowing.
