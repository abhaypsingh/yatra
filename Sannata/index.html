Sannāṭā

the stillness

There is silence, and then there is sannata — the deep, eerie stillness that follows something enormous. The moment after bad news. The house after everyone has gone. The emptiness of a room that used to be full. Sannata asks you to name the times when everything went utterly still — and what you found in the void.

Sannāṭā

Sannata is different from silence. Silence can be peaceful. Sannata is the silence that screams. It is the absence of something that should be there.

The sannata after a loved one leaves the house for the last time. The sannata in your chest when you receive news you were not ready for. The sannata of sitting in an exam hall knowing you do not know the answers.

These voids are terrifying. But they are also the places where the deepest truths reveal themselves. In the sannata, you hear what was always there but too noisy to notice. Name the stillnesses. They changed you more than the noise ever did.
