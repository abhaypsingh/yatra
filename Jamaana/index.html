Jamānā

the age

A jamaana is a whole era — the world as it was in a particular time that no longer exists. It is not just a date on a calendar but an entire atmosphere: the music, the prices, the way people spoke to each other, the things that were possible. Every person carries several jamaanas inside them. Jamānā asks you to name the ages you lived through — the worlds that existed and then vanished.

Jamānā

Elders say “hamaare jamaane mein” — in our time — and what follows is not just nostalgia but the description of an entire world that has ceased to exist. The jamaana of handwritten letters. The jamaana of joint families. The jamaana of walking to school through fields. Each jamaana has its own light, its own speed, its own rules about what mattered.

You have lived through jamaanas too, even if you are young. The world before a loss. The world before a move. The era when a particular group of friends were inseparable. These are not just memories — they are entire civilisations that rose and fell within your single lifetime.

Name the ages. Bear witness to the worlds that lived and died inside you.
