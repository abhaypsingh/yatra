Machān

The machaan is the loneliest structure in Indian agriculture. A farmer climbs up with a lantern and a stick, and sits there all night. Below, the crop he spent months growing. In the darkness, the animals that would eat it in minutes. His only weapon is wakefulness. His only strategy is presence.

You have sat on machaans. The vigils over a relationship that was under threat. The watchful years of early parenthood. The time you stayed alert while others slept because something precious depended on your attention.

Name the vigils. They are the lonely nights that protected the harvest.
