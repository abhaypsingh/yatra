Tavīz

the amulet

A taveez is a small metal locket worn around the neck or arm, containing a prayer, a verse, a folded blessing. You don’t open it. You don’t read what’s inside. You simply trust that it protects. Tavīz asks you to name the invisible protections you carry — the beliefs, habits, people, or words that guard you even though you can’t see how.

Tavīz

Nobody asks what’s written inside a taveez. That would be like unwrapping trust to check if it’s real. The whole point is faith — that something you cannot see is working on your behalf. A grandmother ties it around a child’s arm and says: this will keep you safe. The child grows up and the thread frays, but the feeling of safety remains.

You carry amulets too. A mantra you repeat before something difficult. A person whose voice calms you. A memory you return to when the world feels unsafe. These are your taveez — invisible, folded, sealed.

Name them. Not to open them, but to honour that they protect.
