A naara is a war cry — words hurled skyward with the full force of a body that has had enough. It is the slogan that gathers strangers into a single fist, the shout that cracks the silence before the silence cracks you. Every revolution began with someone deciding their voice was louder than their fear. Nārā

There are words meant to be spoken and words meant to be thrown. A naara is the latter — language stripped of politeness and loaded with conviction. It is the chant on the street corner, the refusal distilled into a phrase short enough to be repeated by thousands. Some naaras topple empires. Some simply give a single person the courage to walk out a door they have been standing behind for years.

The power of a naara is not in its volume but in the moment it erupts. Something has been endured long enough. The throat that has swallowed injustice finally opens. The cry is not eloquent — it does not need to be. It only needs to be louder than the fear that kept it locked inside.
