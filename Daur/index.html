Daur

Each era of a life has its own spirit. The daur of childhood has a different gravity than the daur of middle age. The daur of struggle has different rules than the daur of ease. You cannot see the spirit of an era while you are inside it — only from the outside, looking back, do you recognise its character. Temporal identity is real: who you were in one daur may be unrecognisable to who you are in the next.

You have lived through daurs. Chapters that had their own soundtracks, their own colours, their own logic. Some lasted years. Some lasted months but felt like decades. Each one ended not with a bang but with a slow realisation that the rules had changed.

Name the eras. They are the chapters that gave your life its structure.
