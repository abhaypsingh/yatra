Dastūr

A dastoor arrives before you do. By the time you are old enough to question it, it has already been practised for so long that questioning feels like rebellion. Take off your shoes before entering the house. Touch the feet of your elders. Do not eat before the oldest person at the table has begun. These customs carry the compressed wisdom — and sometimes the compressed prejudice — of generations.

The relationship with dastoor is never simple. Some customs you follow gladly because you feel their truth in your bones. Others you follow reluctantly, out of respect or fear of conflict. And some you eventually break — not easily, not without guilt, but because the custom no longer serves the life you are building. Each choice reveals something about who you are becoming.

Record your customs — the kept and the broken alike. They are the clearest map of where tradition and self-determination meet in your life.
