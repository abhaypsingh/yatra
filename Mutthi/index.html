Muṭṭhī

the fist

A closed fist holds tight — sand, a secret, a resolve. But the tighter you grip, the more slips through the cracks between your fingers. The mutthi is the tension between holding and losing, between control and surrender. Muṭṭhī asks you to name what you held so tightly it left marks on your palms.

Muṭṭhī

There is a children’s game: fill your fist with sand and squeeze. The harder you press, the faster the sand escapes. Open your hand flat and the sand stays. The mutthi teaches what the mind resists — that control is often the enemy of keeping. The things we grip hardest are often the things we lose fastest.

You have clenched your fists around many things. A person you loved and feared losing. An outcome you needed desperately. A version of yourself you refused to let go. The marks on your palms tell the story of what mattered most — and what the gripping cost.

Name what you held. See whether the grip served you or only tightened the loss.
