the shimmer

Jhilmil is the word for things that shimmer — not solid light, not darkness, but the flickering in between. Stars. Sequins on a dupatta. Sunlight through leaves. The uncertain glow of something almost beautiful. Jhilmil asks you to name the shimmers — the things that flickered between real and imagined, between hope and illusion.

A shimmer is honesty in motion. It does not pretend to be steady light. It says: I am here, then I am not, then I am here again. This is closer to truth than anything that claims to be constant.

Happiness shimmers. So does hope. So does love. They are not switch-on, switch-off. They flicker. And the flickering is not weakness — it is the nature of precious things. Even stars shimmer. Even the best days have moments of shadow.

Name the shimmers. They are the honest lights of your life.
