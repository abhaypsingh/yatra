Jawāb

We ask questions and expect answers to come in the same conversation, the same year, the same decade. But life is not a classroom. The deepest questions — why did this happen to me, why did they leave, what am I meant to do — have answers that arrive on their own schedule, often when you have stopped asking.

You have received late answers. The reason behind a parent’s harshness, understood only after becoming a parent yourself. The purpose of a suffering, visible only from the far side of it. These answers did not come through thinking. They came through living.

Name the answers. They are proof that meaning arrives, even when it is not on time.
