the counting

You are always counting. Marks. Followers. Likes. Rank. How many friends came to your birthday. How many times someone texted first. But the things that truly count — depth of friendship, moments of courage, quiet growth — those have no number. Ginti asks: what do you count, and what truly counts?

Gintī

Numbers feel safe. They are clear, they compare, they rank. But numbers lie by omission.

Your mark does not count the nights you could not sleep. Your follower count does not count who would actually sit with you in a hospital. Your rank does not count the ideas you had that did not fit the exam format.

You are not wrong to count. Numbers are one kind of truth. But they are never the whole truth. Name what you count. Then ask what the numbers cannot see.
