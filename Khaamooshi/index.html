Khāmoshī

In Urdu poetry, khaamooshi is not the absence of sound but the presence of everything that cannot be spoken. Ghalib wrote entire ghazals about what the silence between lovers contains. A mother’s silence at the dinner table can carry more weight than any lecture. A friend’s silence on the phone can say I am here, I have nothing to fix, I am just holding this with you.

But silence can also be a blade. The parent who never said I am proud of you. The apology that never came. The truth that everyone in the room knew but no one would name. These silences do not fade — they calcify. They become part of the architecture of a family, a friendship, a self.

Name the silences. Give words to the spaces where words refused to go.
