Svabhāva

true nature

People will tell you who you are. Some will be right. Many will be wrong.

The only voice that gets the final word is yours.

This is a place to write down what you know to be true about yourself — things no one can give you and no one can take away.

This is who I am.

Things I know to be true about myself.

No one gave you these truths. No one can take them. They are yours — written in your own hand, in your own words. Come back whenever you need to remember.

Svabhāva स्वभाव

Svabhāva means one’s own nature, innate disposition, the essence of who you are before the world shapes you. In Indian philosophy, knowing your svabhāva is the foundation of all authentic action.

When you grow up hearing different stories about who you are from different people, it can be hard to know which voice to trust. This practice helps you listen to the one voice that matters most — your own.

Your core document grows over time. Each truth you write is a stone in the foundation of who you are becoming.

Everything stays on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere.
