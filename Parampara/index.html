Paramparā

the unbroken chain

You did not begin with yourself.

Behind you stands a chain of people who sacrificed, survived, and loved so that you could exist. Most of them you will never know. But everything they endured is alive in you right now.

This is the story of a chain. Each link is a generation. Each generation gave up something so the next could have something more.

You are not just yourself. You are the summit of a mountain of love you have never seen.

Seven generations. Seven sacrifices. Each one invisible to the next, each one essential to the one that followed.

You are reading these words on a device that would have seemed like magic to the woman who carried water five miles. You are making choices that the man who worked the fields could not have imagined. You are living the life they dreamed of without knowing what it would look like.

The chain did not break. It held. And now it holds you.

Paramparā परम्परा

Paramparā (Sanskrit: succession, lineage, unbroken chain) is the concept that knowledge, culture, and love are passed from generation to generation in an unbroken stream. In Indian tradition, you are not a solitary individual — you are a link in a chain that stretches back beyond memory.

The generations described here are not one specific family. They are every family. Every person alive today stands at the top of a mountain of sacrifice they did not choose and cannot repay. The only thing they can do is add their own link.

What will yours be?
