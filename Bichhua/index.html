Bichhiyā

There is a famous story about a monk who kept saving a scorpion from drowning, even though it stung him every time. “It is the scorpion’s nature to sting,” he said. “It is my nature to save.”

But you are not a monk. You do not have to keep saving what stings you. Some people sting because they are in pain themselves. Some sting because they can. Knowing the difference is wisdom. Acting on that knowledge is strength.

Name the stings. Not to hold grudges, but to learn where to place your hands more carefully.
