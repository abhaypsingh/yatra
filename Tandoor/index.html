A tandoor runs at extreme heat. Nothing survives inside it unchanged. Dough goes in soft and comes out as naan — charred, puffed, transformed. Tandoor asks you to name the crucibles in your life — the extreme situations that transformed you completely, where you entered as one thing and emerged as another.

Tandūr

The tandoor does not ask permission. It does not care if the dough is ready. The heat is the heat. What enters must either transform or burn. There is no middle ground.

Life has tandoors. The death of someone you loved. A family crisis. A failure so complete that everything you believed about yourself had to be rebuilt from ash. You did not choose to enter. But you came out different. Harder on the outside. Softer within. Changed.

Name the tandoors. The fires you survived. You wear their marks. They are proof of transformation.
