Viraana is the landscape after everything has left — a house emptied of people, a heart emptied of hope, a street where shops once thrived now standing with broken windows. Yet in desolation there is a strange, austere beauty. Vīrānā asks you to walk through the ruins — the places and periods of emptiness in your life where you found, against all expectation, something worth seeing in the barrenness.

Vīrānā

There is a phase in every loss where the grief subsides enough for you to look around and see where you are. The landscape is empty. The people who filled it are gone. The activities that gave it meaning have ceased. You are standing in a viraana — a ruin. And the first instinct is to flee, to fill the emptiness with noise, to rebuild immediately.

But those who stay in the desolation for a while discover something unexpected. In the absence of everything familiar, the self becomes visible. Without the clutter of routine and relationship, you hear your own thoughts for the first time. The ruin becomes a mirror — stark, unflattering, and more honest than any room full of furniture and people.
