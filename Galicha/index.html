Galīchā

When guests come, the galicha comes out. The house is swept. The good crockery appears. A version of your family is presented that is smoother, softer, more beautiful than the everyday. This is not dishonesty. It is care. It is the wish to make others comfortable.

But you do this to yourself too. You carpet over your pain for job interviews. You carpet over your doubts for your parents. You present a smooth surface when underneath the floor is cracked and cold.

Name the carpets. Honour the care they represent. But also check whether you have forgotten what the floor actually looks like.
