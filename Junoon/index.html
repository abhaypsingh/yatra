Junūn

Junoon is the word for a madness that is also a gift. The Sufis used it to describe the divine intoxication of the seeker who abandons everything for the beloved. But junoon lives in ordinary life too — the entrepreneur who cannot stop building, the musician who plays until their fingers bleed, the mother who fights systems that everyone else has accepted.

Society fears junoon because it cannot be controlled or scheduled. It disrupts routines, ignores boundaries, refuses moderation. But almost everything worth remembering was created by someone in the grip of junoon. The question is not whether obsession is dangerous — it always is — but whether what it produces justifies what it burns.

Name your obsessions without apology. They are the truest map of what you are.
