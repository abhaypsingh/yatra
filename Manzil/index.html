Manzil

A destination is not just a place. It is a promise someone made about your future — sometimes without asking you.

Some destinations are given with love: a parent who wants you safe, a teacher who sees potential. Some are imposed: society’s definition of success, the algorithm’s version of a good life.

You are allowed to question the map. You are allowed to say: this road does not feel like mine. You are even allowed to stop and stand still while you figure it out. Name where you are headed. Then ask: is it where I want to go?
