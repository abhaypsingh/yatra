Taksal

Value is conferred, not inherent. A piece of metal becomes a coin only when the taksal stamps it. Before that stamp, it has potential but no currency. So it is with people: your qualities exist as raw material until someone or something recognises them, names them, and gives them a form that the world can trade in. The mint does not create the metal — it shapes it into something that can circulate.

You have been through taksals. A school that shaped your thinking. A mentor who named your talent. A crisis that forged your character. Each one took raw material and stamped it with a value that the world could recognise.

Name the mints. They are the forges that gave your qualities their currency.
