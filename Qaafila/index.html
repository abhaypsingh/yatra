Qāfilā

In the old trade routes, no merchant travelled alone. The desert was too vast, the bandits too many, the nights too cold. So they formed qaafilas — caravans of shared purpose. Each person carried their own load, but the caravan carried everyone. If one camel fell, the others waited. If one traveller fell ill, the group slowed.

Your life has had qaafilas. The cohort you graduated with. The colleagues who survived a difficult year together. The friends who all became parents at the same time and stumbled through it in parallel. These temporary fellowships shaped you as much as any individual relationship — because a qaafila teaches you that the journey matters more than the destination.

Name the caravans you have belonged to. Honour the roads you did not walk alone.
