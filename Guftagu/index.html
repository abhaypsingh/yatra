Guftagū

Guftagu is not chit-chat. It is the Urdu word for a conversation that has depth — the kind that happens when two people stop performing and start speaking from the place where their truths live. It often happens late at night, or during a long walk, or in a crisis when there is no energy left for pretence. The masks come off. The real voices emerge.

You remember these conversations even if you have forgotten thousands of others. The one where someone finally told you what they had been carrying. The one where you said the thing you had been afraid to say. The one that ended a chapter or began one. Words are usually disposable — but some become permanent.

Name the conversations. They are the moments when language did what it was always meant to do.
