Dāstān

the epic tale

A dāstān is a long, layered story told over many nights — part history, part myth, part moral instruction. The dāstān tradition knows that the most important stories cannot be told in one sitting. They must unfold, episode by episode, each night adding a new layer. Dāstān asks you to name the stories told to you that became part of your own story — the narratives you absorbed from family, books, and culture that shaped how you understand your life.

Dāstān

We are made of stories before we are made of experience. A child who has never been to war knows what courage is because of the dāstāns told at bedtime. A young person who has never been betrayed knows what loyalty means because of the tales absorbed from elders. The dāstān is pre-experience: it gives you a framework before life gives you the events.

Your dāstāns were the stories that arrived before the experience and then made the experience legible. Your grandmother’s tale of partition. Your father’s account of his first job. The myth of Sita’s fire. These stories became the lenses through which you read your own life.
