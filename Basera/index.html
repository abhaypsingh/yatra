Baserā

There is wisdom in knowing where to land. The bird does not build a new nest every evening — it finds a branch, a ledge, a sheltered spot, and rests there until morning. The basera is not home. It is the temporary refuge that makes the next day possible. Chosen not for permanence but for the immediate need to be still.

You have had baseras. The person’s house where you showed up uninvited and were always welcome. The quiet corner where you went to recover. The habit or ritual that settled you when nothing else could. These were not solutions. They were resting places.

Name the roosts. They are the refuges that gave you the rest to continue.
