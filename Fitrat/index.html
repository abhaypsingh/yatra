Fitrat

the nature

Fitrat is innate nature — what a person is when all pretence is stripped away. It is the core that remains after circumstances, upbringing, and performance have been peeled back. Fitrat cannot be taught or unlearned; it simply is. Fitrat asks you to name the natures you discovered that could not be changed — in yourself and in others.

Fitrat

There is a saying: fitrat nahi badalti — nature does not change. A generous person remains generous even when they have nothing. A suspicious person remains suspicious even when surrounded by loyalty. Fitrat is deeper than habit, older than choice. It is the grain of the wood, the direction in which a river insists on flowing no matter how many dams are built.

You have spent years trying to change natures — your own and others’. Some efforts succeeded in reshaping behaviour, but the fitrat underneath remained, patient and permanent, waiting to resurface. The wisdom is not in changing nature but in recognising it.

Name the natures. Accept the grain of the wood.
