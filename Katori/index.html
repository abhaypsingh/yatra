the bowl

A bowl can only hold so much. You are the same. You carry schoolwork, friendships, family expectations, your own dreams, worries about the future — and sometimes the bowl overflows. Katori asks you to look at what fills your bowl and ask: what deserves to be here? What can I pour out?

How much can you hold?

Name what fills your bowl

Is it overflowing? Is there room?

Kaṭorī

Nobody teaches you that you have a limit. They just keep adding to your bowl until one day it spills.

The spilling is not your failure. It is the bowl’s honest truth.

You are allowed to say: my bowl is full. You are allowed to pour out something that no longer nourishes you to make room for something that does. Name what is in your bowl. The first step to balance is knowing what you carry.
