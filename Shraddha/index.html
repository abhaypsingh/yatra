Śraddhā

faith in yourself

Not religious faith. Not blind confidence. Something quieter.

The deep trust that you will be okay. That you have survived everything so far. That the person you are becoming is someone you can count on.

Six pieces of evidence. Because faith in yourself is not a feeling. It is a case you build, one proof at a time.

the evidence:

The case is strong.

Six pieces of evidence. Six proofs that you are more capable, more resilient, more worthy of your own trust than you knew. And this is only what you found in a few minutes. The full file is a lifetime long.

Shraddha is not arrogance. It is not pretending you are invincible. It is the quiet, earned knowledge that says: I do not know what is coming. But I know who is meeting it. And that person has a perfect record of surviving hard things.

You have never once failed to make it to today. That is your evidence. That is your faith.

Śraddhā श्रद्धा

Śraddhā means faith, trust, confidence. In the Bhagavad Gita, it is described as the essence of a person — “a person is what their shraddha is.”

Self-efficacy — the psychological term for faith in yourself — is one of the strongest predictors of success, resilience, and well-being. It is not built by positive affirmations. It is built by evidence: remembering what you have already survived and accomplished.

This experience helps you build that evidence file. Not with flattery. With facts.
