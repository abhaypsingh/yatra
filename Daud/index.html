the race

Everyone is running. For marks, for admission, for approval, for a future that keeps moving further away the faster you chase it. You were entered in this race before you could choose. Daud asks you to name the races you are running — and wonder: who set the finish line? Is it even yours?

Dauḍ

The cruelest thing about the race is that nobody remembers entering it. One day you were a child playing. The next day you were a student with a rank, a percentile, a seat number.

Some races are worth running — the race to become kinder, the race to learn something that genuinely excites you. Others are just noise disguised as purpose.

You are allowed to slow down. You are allowed to stop and ask: where am I actually going? And if the only honest answer is “where everyone else is going,” then maybe it is time to find your own track.
