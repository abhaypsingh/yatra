Khaṭal

the cattle shed

A khatal is the low-roofed space where cattle rest — warm with animal breath, thick with the smell of hay and dung. It is unglamorous. But it is where the family’s true wealth lives — the animals that give milk, pull ploughs, and sustain the household. Khaṭal asks you to name the unglamorous responsibilities you tend daily — the duties that sustain everything but receive no admiration.

Khaṭal

Nobody photographs the khatal. Nobody writes poems about it. It smells. The work is repetitive: feed, water, clean, repeat. But take away the khatal and the household collapses. No milk for the children. No dung for fuel. No ox for the field. The glamorous harvest depends entirely on the unglamorous shed.

Your khatals are the invisible responsibilities. The bills you pay on time. The groceries you restock. The emotional labour of checking in on everyone. Work that nobody applauds because nobody notices until it stops.

Name the sheds. They are the unglamorous foundations of everything that matters.
