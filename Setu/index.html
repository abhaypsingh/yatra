the bridge

Between any two people who love each other, there is a bridge.

It is built plank by plank — from listening, from patience,
from showing up, from apologizing, from trying again.

Some planks are yours to lay. Some belong to the other person.
You cannot build the whole bridge alone.

But you can always lay your half. That is where it begins.

Setu सेतु

Setu (Sanskrit: bridge, connection) appears in the ancient epic Ramayana as the bridge built by an army to cross the ocean — each stone laid with devotion, each piece essential. It took a million hands. No single one could do it alone.

In relationships, the bridge between two people is built the same way: plank by plank, through small acts of connection. Some days you lay a plank. Some days the other person does. Some days neither of you can. The bridge waits.

You will never be able to build the whole bridge yourself. But the half you build is a declaration: I am here. I am not leaving. I am doing my part.
