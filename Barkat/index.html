Barkat

There is a kind of abundance that defies arithmetic. Your grandmother cooked for four but fed twelve. Your father’s salary was modest but the house never felt poor. This is barkat — not wealth, but the strange multiplication that happens when something is given with sincerity and received with gratitude.

Barkat cannot be manufactured. It is not profit or surplus. It is the feeling that life has given you more than the numbers suggest. A single conversation that nourishes for weeks. A small act of kindness that ripples outward beyond anyone’s accounting.

Name the blessings that exceeded their container. Honour the abundance that arrived without an invoice.
