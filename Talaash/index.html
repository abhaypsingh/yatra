Talāsh

The search is the most honest thing about being young. You are supposed to be searching. That restless feeling is not a problem — it is the engine of your becoming.

Some searches have answers: the right career, the right friend, the right words for what you feel. Others have no destination — they are the search itself: for meaning, for peace, for who you will become.

Do not rush to stop searching. The search is doing something to you — it is shaping you into someone who asks questions, who looks, who refuses to settle for the first easy answer. Name what you seek.
