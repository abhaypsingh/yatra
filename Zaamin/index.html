Zāmin

To be a zaamin is to say: if this person fails, I will pay. It is the deepest form of trust, because it is trust with consequences. The guarantor stakes not just belief but reputation, honour, sometimes money. It is easy to say “I believe in you.” It is another thing entirely to sign your name beneath that belief.

You have had zaamin in your life — people who vouched for you when you had nothing to show. A parent who co-signed a loan. A mentor who recommended you for a job you were not yet qualified for. And perhaps you have been a zaamin too, putting your name on the line for someone who needed a chance.

Name the guarantors. Their signatures made your beginnings possible.
