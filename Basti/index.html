the settlement

A basti is a cluster of homes — not a city, not a village, but a small settlement where lives press close together. In a basti, everyone knows everyone. Privacy is a luxury; belonging is the default. Bastī asks you to name the settlements in your life — the tight-knit clusters of people where you lived close enough to be known completely, for better and worse.

Bastī

In a basti, the walls are thin and the doors are often open. You hear your neighbour’s arguments, smell their cooking, know when their child is sick. This sounds like an invasion of privacy. But it is also an architecture of care — because when something goes wrong, the entire basti responds. Nobody is alone. Nobody falls without being noticed.

You have lived in bastis. The dorm, the joint family, the close team. Places where hiding was impossible and belonging was involuntary. Where the claustrophobia and the warmth were the same thing.

Name the settlements. They are where you were known before you were ready to be.
