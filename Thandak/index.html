Ṭhanḍak

Coolness is the reward the body gives itself after surviving heat. You know this feeling in your bones — the deep exhale after something terrible ends.

The cool side of the pillow after a fever. The first morning of summer holidays. The silence after a family argument resolves. The lightness when a secret is finally shared.

These moments are medicine. They remind you that every storm passes, every heat breaks, every difficulty has a doorway out. Name the coolness. It is proof that you have survived before and can survive again.
