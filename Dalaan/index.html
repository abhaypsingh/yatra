Dalān

the verandah

A dalaan is the covered verandah at the front of a house — roofed but open-sided, where inside and outside meet. Children do homework here. Elders nap here. Neighbours stop to chat without entering. The dalaan belongs to both the house and the street. Dalān asks you to name the threshold spaces in your life where your private self and public self coexist — the verandahs where you are both home and visible.

Dalān

The dalaan is the most sociable part of an Indian home. It has a roof but no walls. You are sheltered from sun and rain but open to every passerby. The dalaan says: I live here, and I am available. Come, sit, talk. You don’t need to enter my house to enter my company.

Your dalaans are the parts of yourself you make accessible without making yourself fully vulnerable. The warmth you offer without opening every room. The hospitality that has limits but not walls.

Name the verandahs. They are the gracious spaces between privacy and openness.
