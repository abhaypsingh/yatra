Pallū

the saree end

The pallu is the loose end of a saree that drapes over the shoulder. It is the most expressive part — it covers the head in respect, wipes a child’s face, carries small things, and flutters with every step. Pallu asks you to name the pallus — the flowing, graceful, multi-purpose gestures of care that the women in your life performed without thinking.

Pallū

The pallu is the Swiss Army knife of Indian womanhood. With it, a mother covers her head before elders, fans herself in the heat, wraps sweets brought from a neighbour, wipes a tear — her child’s or her own — and creates a makeshift cradle for a baby.

All of this without thinking. The pallu moves as naturally as breathing. It is care made fabric. It is love in the form of six yards of cloth that keeps finding new ways to be useful.

Name the pallus. The quiet, flowing gestures of care that no one notices until they are gone.
