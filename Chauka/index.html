The kitchen is where your family’s love takes its most honest form. The dal your mother makes when you are sick. The chai your father stirs when he does not know what to say. The recipe your grandmother never wrote down because she measured in handfuls. Chauka asks you to name the meals, the smells, the rituals of your kitchen — and the love hiding inside them.

In Indian homes, the kitchen is not a room. It is a language. Your mother speaks it fluently — her love measured in pinches of salt and the time she spends rolling rotis.

You may not notice it now. You will notice it terribly when you leave home and no one makes chai the way it should taste. The kitchen is where your family’s unspoken feelings become something you can hold in your hands.

Name the kitchen rituals. They are more yours than you know. One day you will cook them for someone you love, and the circle will close.
