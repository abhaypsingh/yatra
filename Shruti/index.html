The Unheard

Six conversations. Eighteen layers. Every time, the surface words felt complete — until you listened deeper and found the real message underneath.

Most arguments are not about what they seem to be about. Most silence is not empty. Most anger is not really anger.

Think of someone who says things that frustrate you.

What might they be trying to say that the words can't carry?

Shruti श्रुति

Shruti (Sanskrit: that which is heard) refers in the Vedic tradition to sacred knowledge received through deep listening — not learned, not invented, but heard by those who had cultivated the capacity to receive it.

In human relationships, the deepest truths are rarely spoken directly. We say “I'm fine” when we're breaking. We say “It doesn't matter” when it matters more than anything. We say “Leave me alone” when what we mean is “Please stay.”

This experience teaches the art of listening beneath the surface — to hear not just words, but the meaning they carry and the need they are trying to express.

The people who are hardest to understand are usually the ones who need to be heard the most.
