Ghungrū

the anklet bell

Ghungroo ring when you move. They are the sound of dancing, of not standing still. There is a part of you that wants to dance — to celebrate, to be loud, to take up space, to feel joy without apologising for it. Ghungroo asks you to name the things that make you want to move, to dance, to ring out loud.

Ghungrū

Joy is underrated. In a world that constantly asks you to be serious, productive, and responsible, the simple act of dancing is revolutionary.

Ghungroo do not ring when you are still. They need movement. They need you to not care who is watching. They need your feet to trust the rhythm even when your mind is uncertain.

You are allowed to be joyful. You are allowed to be loud with happiness. Name what makes your ghungroo ring. Then let them.
