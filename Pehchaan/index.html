Pehchān

Identity is not one thing. It is a mosaic — built from the songs you hum, the things that make you angry, the way you hold a book, the hill you would die on.

Most people never stop to list the pieces. They know who they are in a vague way, but not in a specific one. Specifics are where truth lives.

Write down the pieces of yourself. The portrait that emerges will surprise you.

Your identity is stored only on this device.
