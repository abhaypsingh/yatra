Panāh

A refuge is not an escape. Escape is running away. Refuge is running toward — toward safety, toward quiet, toward the version of the world where you do not have to perform or explain yourself.

Your refuge might be a physical place — a bedroom, a library, a tree you climb. It might be a person whose presence alone is enough to calm you. It might be a song, a book, a ritual. It does not matter what form it takes. What matters is that it exists.

Name your refuges. Knowing where they are is knowing how to take care of yourself.
