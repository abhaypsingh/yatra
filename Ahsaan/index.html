Ahsān

In every life there are debts that no currency can settle. Someone took you in when you had nowhere to go. Someone believed in you when you had given up on yourself. Someone sacrificed years of their own life so yours could begin. These are not transactions — they are ahsaan, favours that reshape the topology of a relationship forever.

The weight of ahsaan is peculiar. It does not crush — it anchors. You carry it not as burden but as proof that someone once loved you more than the cost of loving you. The asymmetry is the point. It is not meant to balance.
