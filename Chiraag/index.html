Chirāg

Before electricity, the chiraag was the centre of the home. Families gathered around its small circle of light to eat, to talk, to simply be together. The flame was modest — it could not light the whole room, let alone the street. But it was enough. It meant someone was home, someone was awake, someone had taken the trouble to light the wick.

Your life has had chiraags. A teacher who saw something in you that you could not see in yourself. A friend who called at the exact moment you were about to give up. A sentence in a book that suddenly made sense of years of confusion. These were not floodlights. They were small, warm, flickering — and they were enough to show you the next step.

Name your lamps. Honour the modest lights that saved you from stumbling in the dark.
