Ghāv

A wound is honest. It does not pretend to be fine. It bleeds. It demands attention. It forces you to slow down and tend to yourself. In that way, a wound is a better teacher than comfort.

The deepest wounds are not always visible. A harsh word from someone you love. The death of a dream you held close. The realization that someone you trusted was not who you thought. These wounds bleed inward.

Name the wounds. Not to reopen them, but to acknowledge that they happened, that they mattered, and that you survived them.
