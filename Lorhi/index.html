Before you had words, someone sang to you. A lullaby is not just a song — it is the promise that someone will stay until the dark passes. Think of the things that soothe you now — a voice, a phrase, a routine, a place. The lullabies of your life are not always melodies. Sometimes they are silences that feel safe.

A lullaby does not solve anything. It does not fix the problem or answer the question. It simply says: I am here. You can rest.

As you grow older, the lullabies change. Instead of a song, it might be a friend who listens without giving advice. A cup of tea made by someone who noticed you were tired. A familiar route you walk when you need to think.

Name the things that calm your noise. They are more important than you think.
