Ṭhikānā

An address is where people can find you. But your real address — the place where you can find yourself — is rarely a location on a map.

It might be the feeling of reading a book on a rainy afternoon. It might be your grandmother’s lap when you were small. It might be a future place you have not found yet but can feel pulling you forward.

Belonging is not always about geography. Sometimes it is about a moment, a person, a state of mind. Name your true addresses. They are the coordinates of your soul.
