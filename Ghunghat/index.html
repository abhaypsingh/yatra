Ghunghaṭ

the veil

A ghunghat is the edge of a saree or dupatta drawn over the face — not to hide, but to choose what is revealed and to whom. It is selective visibility. A woman behind a ghunghat sees everything; it is the world whose view is filtered. Ghunghaṭ asks you to name the veils you wear — the things you let the world see, and the things you keep behind the cloth.

What do you veil from the world?

Ghunghaṭ

The ghunghat is often misunderstood as oppression. Sometimes it is. But sometimes it is power — the power to decide who sees you fully. The woman behind the ghunghat is not absent. She is present and choosing. She sees clearly while others see only cloth.

You wear ghunghats every day. The professional composure that veils your exhaustion. The smile that covers your anxiety. The silence that hides your real opinion. These veils are not lies — they are choices about visibility.

Name the veils. Know what you hide, and know that you choose to.
