Rāz

Every family has a raaz. Every person carries at least one. The secret is a strange organism — it grows heavier the longer it is held, yet the thought of releasing it feels more dangerous than the weight. Some secrets protect the innocent. Others protect the guilty. Most protect a version of reality that everyone has agreed to believe.

Then there are the secrets discovered — the letter found in a drawer after someone dies, the conversation overheard that reframes an entire childhood, the confession that arrives decades too late. These discoveries do not just add new information; they rearrange everything you thought you knew.

You do not need to reveal your secrets here. Simply name them — acknowledge their existence and their weight. Even the act of writing “this exists” can lighten the load.
