Chiṛiyā

the bird

There is something wild inside you. A part that does not fit the timetable, the plan, the careful life others have designed. It wants to fly — to create, to wander, to do the impractical beautiful thing. Chiriya asks you to name the wild bird inside you — the dreams, urges, and instincts that do not fit the cage of the expected life.

▮

Chiṛiyā

Every person has a bird inside them. A wild, restless thing that does not care about practicality or propriety or five-year plans.

Maybe yours wants to paint instead of study. Maybe it wants to travel with no itinerary. Maybe it wants to love someone the world says is wrong. Maybe it simply wants to sit in a field and do nothing at all.

You do not have to free every bird today. But you must know it is there. A caged bird that is forgotten stops singing. A caged bird that is acknowledged keeps its song alive until the door opens. Name the bird. Let it know you have not forgotten.
