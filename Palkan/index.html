the eyelids

Your eyelids are the only curtain you truly control. You can open them to see beauty, injustice, truth. You can close them to rest, to hide, to pretend you did not notice. Palkan asks you to name what you have chosen to see — and what you have chosen to close your eyes to. Both choices have shaped you.

Seeing is a choice. Not in the optical sense, but in the moral one. You saw that classmate sitting alone and chose to notice or not. You saw your parent’s exhaustion and chose to acknowledge it or look away.

Closing your eyes is not always cowardice. Sometimes it is self-preservation — you cannot look at everything all the time without breaking. But opening them at the right moment is the beginning of courage.

Name what you have seen and what you have turned away from. Both are part of your story. Both have consequences.
