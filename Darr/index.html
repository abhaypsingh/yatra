Fear is not the opposite of courage. Courage is what happens when you feel the fear and move anyway. Every brave thing you have ever done started with being afraid.

Some fears protect you — the fear of fire keeps your hand from the flame. Some fears limit you — the fear of embarrassment keeps your hand down in class. The trick is not to eliminate fear, but to know it well enough to tell the difference.

Name your fears. Not to defeat them, but to understand them. A fear that is named loses some of its power.
