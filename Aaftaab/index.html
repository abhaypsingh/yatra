Āftāb

In the middle of the worst night of your life, someone could have told you that dawn was coming and you would not have believed them. Darkness, when it is total, feels permanent. It rewrites the rules of the universe — convincing you that light was the anomaly and this blackness is the truth. And then, without permission, without negotiation, the sky begins to lighten at the edges.

The aaftaab does not arrive because you earned it or prayed hard enough. It arrives because it always does. And the relief is not just that the darkness is over — it is the discovery that you survived it. That you are still here, blinking, breathing, squinting into a brightness that feels like forgiveness.

Name the suns that rose in your life. Each one is evidence that the night, no matter how long, was never the final chapter.
