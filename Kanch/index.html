the glass

Some things are glass. Trust. Innocence. A reputation. A first impression. They are clear, beautiful, useful — and once shattered, they can never be made perfectly whole again. The cracks will always show. Kanch asks you to name the glass things in your life — what has cracked, what you are protecting, and what beauty lives in the breakage.

Glass is honest. It shows you exactly what is on the other side. And when it breaks, it does not pretend to be whole.

Trust is glass. So is the belief that the world is fair. So is the image of a parent as all-knowing. These things crack, and the cracks change everything.

But cracked glass is not useless. It catches light differently. It creates patterns a whole pane never could. Name the glass things. Some are worth repairing. Some are more beautiful broken.
