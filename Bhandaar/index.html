Bhandār

the storehouse

A bhandaar is the room in every Indian home where provisions are kept — sacks of grain, jars of pickle, tins of ghee. It is abundance stored against an uncertain future. The bhandaar says: we have enough, and we will have enough. Bhandār asks you to name the reserves you have built inside yourself — the emotional, spiritual, and experiential stores that sustain you when times are lean.

What reserves sustain you in lean times?

Bhandār

In every grandmother’s house, the bhandaar was a kind of temple. You entered quietly. You took only what you needed. And somehow it was always full — not because it magically refilled, but because someone was always quietly restocking it. That someone understood: abundance is not about having more than you need. It is about never letting the stores run empty.

You have a bhandaar inside you. The patience stored from years of waiting. The confidence built from small wins. The faith deposited by people who believed in you before you believed in yourself.

Name the stores. Know what sustains you when the harvest is poor.
