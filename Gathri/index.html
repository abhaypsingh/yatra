Gaṭhrī

Everyone carries a bundle. Some of it is useful — the values your family gave you, the lessons you absorbed without trying. Some of it is heavy — expectations that do not fit, fears that were never yours, guilt you inherited.

You did not pack this bundle yourself. It was handed to you, one piece at a time, by the people and places that shaped you. But now it is yours to carry, and yours to sort through.

Open it. Name what is inside. Decide what to keep and what to gently set down.
