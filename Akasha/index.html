the sky

You are not alone. Even when it feels like it.

There are people who shaped you — who taught you something, protected you, made you laugh, or simply stayed. Some are still here. Some have gone. All of them left light inside you.

Name them. One by one. Each person becomes a star. Over time, you will build a sky full of everyone who matters — a constellation of belonging.

Even in the darkest night, you carry an entire sky of people who loved you.

Ākāsha आकाश

Ākāsha (Sanskrit: sky, space, ether) is the fifth element in Hindu philosophy — the vast, unchanging space that holds everything. It is the container within which all of creation exists.

Your sky is not empty. It is filled with everyone who shaped you. Some you see every day. Some you have not spoken to in years. Some are gone. But their light is still in you, still shining, still part of the sky you carry.

All stars are stored on this device alone. This sky belongs to no one but you.
