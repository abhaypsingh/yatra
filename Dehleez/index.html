A threshold is the strip of wood at the bottom of a doorway. You stand on it for only a moment — but in that moment, everything is possible. You are still who you were, and already becoming who you will be.

Growing up is a series of thresholds. The night before your birthday. The walk to a stage. The pause before you say something that will change a friendship. Most people rush past them. But the threshold is where the real feeling lives.
