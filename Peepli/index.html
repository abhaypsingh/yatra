Pīplī

Scientists say the pipal leaf trembles because of its flat stalk and aerodynamic shape. But the old stories say differently: the pipal trembles because it listens. It hears what the wind carries — prayers, sorrows, whispers from far away — and it cannot help but respond. That is why it is planted at crossroads, where all paths meet and all stories pass through.

You have pipal-leaf sensitivities. The part of you that feels the room shift before anyone speaks. The awareness that picks up on what is unsaid. The trembling that is not weakness but receptivity.

Name the leaves. They are your finest instruments of perception.
