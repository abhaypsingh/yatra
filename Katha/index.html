Kathā

the story

The objects in your life have been watching you longer than you know.

The kitchen table heard every argument and every apology. The front door felt every leaving and every return. The old photograph remembers what you have forgotten.

What if they could speak? What would they tell you about the family they have witnessed — and the love they saw, even when you could not?

Everything around you has a story. Most of them are about you.

The Silent Witnesses

Choose an object. Hear its story.

Kathā कथा

Kathā (Sanskrit: story, narrative) is the ancient art of storytelling. In Indian tradition, stories are not entertainment — they are mirrors held at unexpected angles, showing us what we could not see head-on.

These stories are told by the objects that have witnessed family life in silence. They saw the love, the leaving, the trying, and the returning. They remember what we have forgotten, and they forgive what we cannot.

Read slowly. The objects have been patient. They can wait a little longer for you to hear them.
