Tāsh

Nobody chooses their hand. You are dealt a family, a body, a country, a time in history, a set of circumstances you did not request. The game of taash begins not when the cards are shuffled but when you pick them up and decide what to do. Agency lives not in the deal but in the play.

Some hands look strong and are played weakly. Some hands look hopeless and are played with such skill that everyone at the table is stunned. The cards matter less than the player.

Name the hands. Not to complain about what you were given, but to honour how you chose to play.
