Tāl ताल

Tāl (also tāla) means rhythm, beat, clap. In Indian classical music, tāl is the rhythmic framework — the heartbeat beneath the melody. It is measured in claps, waves, and silence.

Tap the screen. Each tap creates a sound and a ripple. The rhythm you build is echoed back to you. There is no score, no right pattern. Just you and the beat.

Everything runs locally. Nothing is tracked or sent anywhere.
