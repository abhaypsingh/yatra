Jālī

The jaalis of Mughal architecture are masterpieces of geometry. They turn a solid wall into a breathing, seeing, beautiful boundary. Not open, not closed. Something in between.

You live behind jaalis every day. The persona you show online — partially revealing, partially concealing. The way you participate in a group — present but guarded. The way you let certain people see certain parts of you. Every relationship has a different jaali pattern.

Name the lattices. Some are beautiful and necessary. Some are keeping you from the light you need.
