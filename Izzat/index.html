Izzat

the respect

Izzat is respect — but two kinds live inside the word. There is the respect earned by living with integrity, and the respect demanded simply because of position or status. One is a garden tended daily; the other is a wall built to hide what’s behind it. Izzat asks you to name the respects that were earned and the ones that were merely demanded.

Izzat

Izzat is the currency of South Asian life. Families are built on it, marriages are arranged around it, feuds are fought over it. But the word contains a deep contradiction: the izzat that comes from genuine character and the izzat that comes from fear or social standing look the same from the outside. Only those close enough can tell which is which.

You have known both kinds. People whose quiet dignity commanded respect without a single demand. And people whose position required you to bow, though nothing inside you agreed. You have also earned respect — and perhaps demanded it when you should have earned it instead.

Name the respects. Learn which ones stand on their own and which ones need a pedestal.
