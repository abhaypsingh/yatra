Okhlī

the mortar

An okhli is a stone mortar where spices are ground by hand. The pestle comes down again and again, and what was whole becomes powder. What was hard becomes soft. What was hidden — the fragrance, the oil, the flavour — is released. Okhli asks you to name the grinding — the repetitive pressures that broke something open and released what was inside.

A spice that is whole is closed. Its flavour is locked inside. Only when the pestle meets the mortar — again and again, with pressure and rhythm — does the spice surrender its essence. The kitchen fills with fragrance only after the grinding.

You have been ground too. By exams. By expectations. By the daily repetition of things you did not want to do. And slowly, through that grinding, something was released. Patience. Resilience. A skill you did not know you had. A fragrance that was always there but needed pressure to emerge.

Name the grindings. They hurt. But they made you aromatic.
