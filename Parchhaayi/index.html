the reflection in others

The qualities you notice in other people are often the ones you carry yourself. When you admire someone’s courage, it is because you recognise it. When someone’s selfishness bothers you — look closer.

Other people are mirrors. What you love in them is often what you value in yourself. What irritates you is often what you have not made peace with in yourself.

This is not always comfortable. But it is always useful. The people who trigger you the most have the most to teach you.

Write what you see in others. Then ask: is this about them, or about me?
