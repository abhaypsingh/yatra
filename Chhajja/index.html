Chhajjā

the overhang

A chhajja is the stone or concrete overhang that juts out above windows and doors in Indian buildings. It does not keep the rain out completely — it just keeps it from hitting you directly. A small extension that makes a large difference. Chhajjā asks you to name the small overhangs in your life — the modest protections that didn’t solve the storm but made it survivable.

Chhajjā

The chhajja is architecture’s most modest hero. It does not enclose you. It does not promise you won’t get wet. It simply extends itself — two feet of stone reaching out from the wall — and in that small extension, the difference between being drenched and being merely damp. Between the rain hitting your face and the rain hitting the ground before you.

Your life has chhajjas. The friend who didn’t solve your problem but stood nearby. The small daily routine that didn’t cure your anxiety but kept it from drowning you. The partial solution that was enough.

Name the overhangs. Sometimes two feet of shelter is all you need.
