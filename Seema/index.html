Sīmā

You are a citizen of many small countries — your family, your friend group, your classroom, your online world. Each has its own language, its own rules, its own version of you.

The border crossing happens a hundred times a day. You adjust your voice, your posture, your vocabulary. Most of the time you do not notice. But sometimes the crossing is hard — when the person your parents want you to be clashes with who your friends know.

There is nothing wrong with being different in different places. But name the borders. Know where one country ends and another begins. That knowledge is freedom.
