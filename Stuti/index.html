Stuti

praise

Gratitude is not about pretending everything is fine. It is about seeing clearly enough to notice what is still good even when much is broken.

Each day, name three things: one person who matters, one moment that was real, and one ordinary thing you would miss if it vanished.

Write why. Not just what — why. The why is where the feeling lives.

What you pay attention to grows. Pay attention to what is good.

Stuti स्तुति

Stuti (Sanskrit: praise, hymn of gratitude) is the act of naming what is good. Not to ignore what is painful, but to ensure that pain does not become the only thing you see.

Three threads each day — a person, a moment, an ordinary thing — woven into a tapestry that grows. When you look back at weeks and months of gratitude, you will see something you could not see one day at a time: that your life was richer than you knew.
