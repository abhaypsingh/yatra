A gaanth is tied to remember. In a dupatta, in a handkerchief, in a thread — a knot says: do not forget this. But knots also form in muscles, in stomachs, in relationships. Gaanth asks you to name the knots — the ones tied to remember, the ones that tighten with tension, and the ones that refuse to come undone.

Gānṭh

In a wedding, the groom’s scarf is tied to the bride’s saree. In a promise, a thread is tied on the wrist. In memory, a knot is tied in the pallu. The gaanth is Indian culture’s way of making the invisible physical.

But gaanths also form without permission. The knot in your stomach before results. The knot of resentment toward someone who hurt you. The knot of unresolved grief that tightens when a certain song plays. These knots carry information. They are your body’s filing system.

Name the knots. Some need to be preserved. Some need to be patiently, carefully untied.
