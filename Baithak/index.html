Baiṭhak

the sitting room

A baithak is the front room of a traditional Indian home — the room where guests are received, where the family presents its best face. It is formal but warm. Cushions on the floor, a clean white sheet, framed photographs on the wall. The baithak is performance and hospitality at once. Baiṭhak asks you to name the sitting rooms in your life — the versions of yourself you present when the world comes calling.

Baiṭhak

Every Indian home has a baithak, even if it doesn’t call it that. The room that is always clean. The sofa no one sits on except when guests come. The cupboard with the good china. The baithak is not dishonest — it is hospitable. It says: we prepared for you. We thought you were worth the effort of our best presentation.

You have baithaks. The résumé version of yourself. The calm voice you use in meetings. The home you rush to tidy before someone visits. These front rooms are not lies — they are the effort you make for others. But the real life happens in the back rooms.

Name the sitting rooms. Know what you display, and what waits behind the curtain.
