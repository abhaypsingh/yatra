Chīkh

You have been trained to be quiet. To not make a scene. To adjust. To compromise. To swallow. And you have — you have swallowed so much that sometimes your chest aches with the weight of unspoken screams.

This is not healthy. Screams that are never released do not evaporate. They compress. They become headaches, sleeplessness, sudden tears at nothing, rage at everything.

You do not have to scream in public. But you need to name the scream. Write it down. Say it to your pillow. Let the words exist somewhere outside your body. That alone is a form of freedom.

Your screams are stored only on this device.
