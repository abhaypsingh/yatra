Chabutrā

the platform

A chabutra is a raised stone or brick platform outside a house — not inside, not on the street, but in between. It is where the old men sat in the evening, where grain was dried in the sun, where children played while adults talked. It belongs to no one and everyone. Chabutrā asks you to name the raised platforms in your life — the in-between spaces where community happened without invitation.

Chabutrā

The chabutra was democracy before democracy had a name. No one owned it. No one was excluded from it. The richest man in the village and the poorest sat on the same stone. News was shared there. Disputes were settled there. Marriages were arranged and griefs were mourned — all on a simple raised platform that was neither home nor road.

You have known chabutras. The cafeteria table where hierarchies dissolved. The stairwell where real talk happened. The parking lot after a meeting where the actual decisions were made.

Name the platforms. They are where your truest community lives.
