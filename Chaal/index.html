In chess, every move opens some paths and closes others. The same is true in life. Choosing one friend group means spending less time with another. Saying yes to one thing means saying no to something else.

Not every move needs to be perfect. Some of the best moves are the ones you make without overthinking — the spontaneous yes, the brave no, the apology you almost did not give.

Name your moves. The ones you are proud of and the ones that haunt you. Both are part of how you play.
