A kambal is rough, heavy, and warm. It is not elegant like a quilt. It does not have patterns or embroidery. But on a cold night, nothing else will do. Kambal asks you to name the blankets in your life — the rough, unglamorous things that kept you warm when it mattered most.

Kambal

The kambal is the opposite of luxury. It is utility. It is survival. On a winter night in a village, when the electricity is out and the wind is howling, the kambal does not care about aesthetics. It wraps around you and says: I will keep you alive until morning.

The most important people and things in your life might be kambals. Not glamorous. Not Instagram-worthy. Just reliably, stubbornly warm. The parent who is not cool but is always there. The habit that is not exciting but keeps you sane. The friend who will never be the life of the party but will drive you to the hospital at 3 a.m.

Name the blankets. They deserve more gratitude than they usually receive.
