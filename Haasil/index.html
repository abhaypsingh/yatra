Hāsil

At the end of any effort, there is a moment of accounting. You set out to achieve something — a degree, a reconciliation, a sum of money, a transformation. The haasil is what you actually hold when the dust settles. It is rarely exactly what you imagined. Sometimes it is less. Sometimes, strangely, it is more — but in a form you did not anticipate.

The most important haasil is often invisible. You went through a difficult year and gained no promotion, no accolade, no visible reward — but you gained the knowledge that you can survive. That survival itself becomes a kind of wealth that funds every future risk.

Be honest about your gains. Not what you told others you achieved, but what you know, quietly, you actually received.
