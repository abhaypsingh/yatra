Saṅkalpa

sacred resolve

Not goals. Not wishes. Not resolutions you will forget by February.

A sankalpa is a vow you make to yourself about who you intend to be. Not what you will achieve — but how you will show up.

Six resolves. Each one a flame you light inside yourself.

why it matters:

Six flames. All yours.

You did not make promises to anyone else. You made them to yourself. And that is the hardest kind of promise to keep — because no one is watching, no one is grading, and the only person you answer to is the one in the mirror.

You will break these resolves. That is not failure. Failure is never making them at all. The resolve is not in being perfect. It is in returning — again and again — to the person you intend to be.

You are not who you were yesterday. You are who you choose to be today. Choose well.

Saṅkalpa संकल्प

Saṅkalpa means a solemn vow or intention formed by the heart and mind together. In yoga and Vedic tradition, a sankalpa is set at the beginning of practice — a seed planted in the deepest part of the self that grows through repetition and commitment.

Unlike goals, which are about outcomes, a sankalpa is about identity. Not what you will do, but who you will be. Research in psychology confirms that identity-based intentions (“I am someone who…”) are far more powerful than outcome-based goals (“I will achieve…”).

The flames you light here are yours to carry.

Everything stays on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere.
