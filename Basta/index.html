the school bag

Every morning you packed a bag and walked into the world. Books, tiffin, water bottle — and invisible things too. Anxiety about a test. Excitement about seeing a friend. The leftover weight of something that happened at home. Basta asks you to name what you carry into the world each day — the visible and the invisible.

Bastā

A child’s school bag is always heavier than it should be. This is true in both senses.

The books are heavy enough. But the invisible weight — the need to perform, the worry about fitting in, the unfinished argument from breakfast, the dread of a particular class — that is what really strains the shoulders.

No one checks the invisible contents of your bag. No one asks what you are carrying besides textbooks. Name what is in your basta. The first step to lightening the load is knowing what is in it.
