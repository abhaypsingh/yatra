Saudāgar

The saudaagar of the old bazaars understood something that modern economics has forgotten: every transaction is a relationship. A merchant who cheated once lost a customer for life and a reputation in the community. The real currency was not gold — it was trust. The handshake meant more than the contract.

You are a merchant too, though you may not call yourself one. You trade your hours for a salary. You trade your silence for a relationship’s survival. You trade your ambitions for your children’s comfort. Some of these trades were wise. Some left you bankrupt in ways that only you can see. The saudaagar’s art is knowing the true cost of what is exchanged.

Audit your trades. Name what you gave and what you got, and decide whether the books balance.
