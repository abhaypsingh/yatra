A stone is the most honest thing in the world. It does not pretend to be light. It does not apologise for its weight.

Some stones you carry by choice — the responsibility of caring for a sibling, the discipline of practice. These are building stones. They make you stronger.

Other stones were placed on you without permission — a grudge, a guilt, a grief that no one helped you process. These stones are not building anything. They are just heavy. Name your stones. Then decide which ones to build with and which ones to set down.
