Ujālā

In the villages, ujala is the word for the first light that enters the house before the sun has fully risen. It is not bright enough to read by, but it is bright enough to see. It is the promise that arrives before the fulfilment. Mothers wake in this light. Tea is made in this light. The day has not yet demanded anything — it is still offering.

Your life has known this brightness. The moment after months of grief when you laughed without guilt. The conversation that finally untangled a confusion you had carried for years. The morning you woke and realised the worst was behind you — not because someone told you, but because something inside you had shifted. These dawns did not arrive with fanfare. They arrived quietly, the way real light does.

Name the brightness. It deserves to be remembered as much as the darkness it followed.
