Anitya

impermanence

Everything you love will change. This is not a tragedy. It is what makes love real.

Six meditations on things that do not last. A sandcastle. A sunset. A friendship. A season. A memory. A face you love.

Read slowly. Let each one fade. What remains is not the thing itself — but what it taught you about being alive.

Everything fades.

The sandcastle. The sunset. The friendship. The season. The memory. The face.

But the love that made you build, watch, hold, wait, remember, and look — that does not fade. That is yours.

Impermanence is not the enemy of love. It is the reason love matters.

Anitya अनित्य

Anitya (Pali: anicca) means impermanence — the truth that all conditioned things arise, exist, and pass away. In Buddhist and Hindu philosophy, this is not pessimism but liberation: when you stop clinging to permanence, you learn to love what is here now.

These six meditations are invitations to sit with impermanence — not as loss, but as the quality that makes everything precious.

Nothing is tracked. Nothing is sent. This is yours alone.
