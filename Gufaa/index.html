the cave

A gufā is a cave — a space carved by water or earth that exists beneath the surface. Sages retreated to caves for meditation. Animals shelter in caves through winter. The cave is where you go when the surface world becomes too much and only darkness will do. Gufā asks you to name the caves you entered — the retreats into solitude, silence, or darkness where you found something that could only be discovered by going below the surface.

Gufā

The cave is the oldest shelter. Before there were houses, there were caves. And there is something the cave offers that no built structure can: total enclosure by the earth itself. In a cave, the world above disappears. Sound changes. Time changes. The darkness is complete and ancient. This is why sages chose caves — not as punishment but as the ideal conditions for encountering what lies deepest.

Your caves were the chosen or forced descents. The depression that took you underground. The period of withdrawal that others called isolation but you knew was excavation. The silence that, from outside, looked like emptiness but from inside was full of discovery. Not all caves are prisons. Some are mines.

Name the caves. What you brought back from the darkness could not have been found anywhere else.
