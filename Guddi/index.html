the kite

A guddi flies because of two opposing forces — the wind that lifts it and the string that holds it. Cut the string and the kite falls. Hold too tight and it cannot soar. The art is in the tension. Guddī asks you to name the things in your life that fly because you hold them with just the right tension — the freedoms that need a thread.

Guddī

On rooftops across India, during Makar Sankranti, the sky fills with kites. Each one looks free. But look down and there is always a hand, always a string, always someone reading the wind and adjusting. The kite thinks it is flying. The flyer knows it is a partnership.

Your life has guddi moments. The relationships that soar because of the invisible string of commitment. The ambitions that fly because discipline holds them. The children who rise because you knew when to pull and when to let out more thread.

Name the kites. They are the beautiful tensions you manage every day.
