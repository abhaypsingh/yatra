Jhulsā

There is a difference between being burned and being scorched. To be burned is to be consumed. To be scorched is to survive the closeness of fire. The roti with dark spots is not ruined — it is the one with the most flavour. The skin that healed from a scald carries the memory of heat, which is also the memory of caution.

Your scorches are your proximity to destruction. The moments you almost lost something vital. The encounters with intensity that left their mark but not their ruin. These marks are not scars — they are evidence that you can withstand heat.

Name the scorches. They are proof you stood close to the fire and lived.
