the signature

Your signature is the one mark the world accepts as proof of you. You practised it in notebook margins, tried different styles, settled on one that felt like yours. Dastakhat asks you to name the signatures — not just the written ones, but all the marks you leave that say: this is mine, I was here, I chose this.

What marks say “this is me”?

Dastakhat

A signature is identity compressed into a gesture. It is the one mark no one else can make. You spent years refining it — in the backs of textbooks, on rough paper, trying to make it look grown-up, trying to make it look like you.

But your real signatures are not written. The way you enter a room. The way you laugh. The thing people think of when they hear your name. The work you do that could only have been done by you. These are dastakhats too.

Name the signatures. The marks that are unmistakably, irreducibly yours.
