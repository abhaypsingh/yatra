the skylight

The small opening in the ceiling of old houses that lets in a shaft of light from directly above. Unlike a window, it shows only sky. No landscape, no neighbours, no world — just light falling straight down. Roshandān asks you to name the skylights — the moments when light fell on you from directly above.

Roshandān

Vertical illumination is different from horizontal light. A window shows you the world. A roshandaan shows you the sky. It is light from an unexpected direction — not from the side, where you can see its source, but from above, where you cannot. This is what grace feels like: light that arrives without explanation, from a direction you were not watching.

You have stood in roshandaan light. Moments when clarity came unbidden. When understanding fell on you without effort. When something you had struggled to see was suddenly illuminated from above, and all you had to do was look up.

Name the skylights. They are the openings through which grace found you.
