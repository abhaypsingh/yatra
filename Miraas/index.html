Mirās

The most significant inheritances are never written in wills. They travel through blood, through repetition, through the silent curriculum of watching your parents live. You inherited your father’s way of going quiet when he was hurt. You inherited your mother’s instinct to feed anyone who walked through the door. You inherited traumas that happened before your birth — fears carried in the body like heirlooms nobody wanted.

But you also inherited resilience. A grandmother’s refusal to give up. An uncle’s dark humour that made unbearable things survivable. The ability to cook a meal from almost nothing — passed down through kitchens you never saw, by women whose names you may not know.

Catalogue your inheritance — the beautiful and the burdensome. Understanding what was passed down is the first step in choosing what to pass on.
