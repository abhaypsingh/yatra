Akshara अक्षर

Akshara (Sanskrit: imperishable letter) means that which cannot be destroyed. In Indian philosophy, akshara represents the eternal, unchanging essence beneath all that changes.

Some letters are meant to be sent. These are the other kind — the ones that exist because they needed to be written. Letters to people you cannot reach. Words you have carried too long.

Everything you write here stays on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere. When you are ready to let go of a letter, you can release it. The words dissolve into light. What they carried is set free.
