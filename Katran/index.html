It is a fragment preserved because it carries the essence of the whole. Katran

Every Indian household has a drawer of katrans. Newspaper articles yellow with age. Fabric pieces from a wedding outfit long outgrown. Recipe cards in a grandmother’s handwriting. These fragments are not the thing itself — they are the memory of the thing. And somehow the fragment is often more precious than the original, because it was chosen. Someone looked at the whole and said: this part. This is what I need to keep.

You have katrans too. A sentence from a conversation that you repeat to yourself. A moment from a year you otherwise can’t recall. A gesture from a person you barely knew. Small clippings from a large life.

Name the clippings. They are the fragments that hold your whole story.
