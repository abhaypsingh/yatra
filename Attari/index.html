An attari is the highest room in a traditional Indian home — a small chamber on the terrace, used to watch for arrivals, to scan the horizon, to keep vigil. From the attari, you see farther than anyone in the house. Aṭṭārī

The attari was the room of vigilance. In the old cities, a woman would climb to the attari to watch for her husband returning from travel. A mother would scan the lanes for her children at dusk. The attari offered no comfort — it was small, hot, exposed to wind — but it offered sight. And sight is its own comfort.

You have climbed to attaris. The moments of clarity after confusion. The perspective that comes only from elevation — from stepping above the noise to see the full landscape. These high places are lonely, but they are where you see the truth.
