Umang

Umang is joy in its most forward-looking form. It is not the satisfaction of having received something but the thrill of knowing something is coming. Children feel it most purely — the night before Eid, the morning of a birthday, the hour before guests arrive. Adults feel it less often, and this scarcity makes it precious.

As you age, umang becomes rarer because experience teaches you that anticipation often exceeds reality. But this is precisely why umang is worth honouring — because the feeling itself, regardless of what follows, is one of the purest forms of being alive. Your heart racing, your plans forming, your smile arriving before the event does.

Recall the moments of umang. Let yourself feel, even in memory, that electric lightness of something wonderful approaching.
