Kachchā

the unfinished

Kachcha means raw, unfinished, unfired. A kachcha house is made of mud — not permanent but alive, breathing, connected to the earth. A kachcha road has no tar. A kachcha person has not yet hardened. Kachcha asks you to name the unfinished things — the parts of you and your life that are still raw, still becoming, still soft enough to reshape.

The world wants you to be pakka — finished, solid, certain. But there is beauty in kachcha. A kachcha house breathes. A kachcha road absorbs rain. A kachcha person can still change.

You are kachcha in more ways than you admit. Your beliefs are still forming. Your skills are still developing. Your understanding of love, justice, and yourself is still raw clay on the wheel. This is not immaturity. This is possibility.

Name the kachcha things. Honour the rawness. Once fired, clay cannot be reshaped. While you are still kachcha, you can become anything.
