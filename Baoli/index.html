A baoli is a stepwell — an inverted temple, descending into the earth to reach water. You must go down to find what sustains life. The deeper you descend, the cooler and darker it gets, until at the bottom you find the water that was always there, waiting. Bāolī asks you to name the descents you made to reach what was hidden.

Bāolī

The stepwell is an architecture of descent. While temples rise, the baoli goes down. Step by step, level by level, you leave the heat and noise of the surface and enter a cooler, quieter world. The light dims. The walls close in. And at the very bottom, you find the water — still, dark, and sustaining. It was always there. You simply had to be willing to go down to reach it.

The deepest truths of a life are found the same way. Not by climbing but by descending. Into grief, into memory, into the questions you avoided. The descent is uncomfortable, but the water at the bottom is what sustains.
