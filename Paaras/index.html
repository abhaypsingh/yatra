Pāras

the philosopher's stone

Pāras is the mythical philosopher’s stone of Indian alchemy — the substance that, by mere touch, turns iron into gold. It does not melt or reshape; it transforms by contact alone. The iron does not know it is about to become gold. The pāras does not labour over the conversion. Touch, and it is done. Pāras asks you to name the touches — people, moments, words — that turned something ordinary in you into something precious, simply by making contact.

Pāras

The legend says that pāras does not discriminate. It does not choose worthy iron and reject unworthy iron. Whatever iron touches it becomes gold. The transformation is not earned; it is received. This is the mystery of certain encounters: you did nothing to deserve them, yet they changed everything. A grandmother’s patience. A mentor’s single question. A book opened at random to the exact page you needed.

Your philosopher’s stones were the contacts that transmuted you. Before the touch, you were one substance. After it, another. You may not have noticed the moment of conversion — iron rarely watches itself becoming gold. But looking back, the difference is unmistakable.

Name the touches. They did not make you something you were not — they revealed the gold that was always latent in the iron.
