Ulfat asks you to notice the affections you have received and given — the gentle, undramatic tenderness that held your life together without ever asking to be noticed.

Ulfat

The most sustaining love is often the least dramatic. It is the parent who checked your blanket every night without waking you. The friend who always texted back, even when the conversation was trivial. The neighbour who left fruit at your door during the months you could not afford it, and never mentioned it, and never will.

Ulfat does not perform. It does not need to be witnessed or reciprocated to exist. It is the affection that operates below the threshold of notice — steady, undemanding, and so reliable that you only recognise its enormity when it is gone. It is the warmth you took for granted because it was always there, like the sun.

Name the affections that sustained you. They are the evidence that you were loved in the way that matters most — consistently, quietly, without condition.
