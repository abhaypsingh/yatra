Pīṭh

You know someone’s back as well as their face. Your mother’s back as she cooked. Your father’s back as he left for work. A friend’s back as they walked away after a fight.

Turning your back is one of the most powerful things a body can do. It can mean: I am done. It can mean: I am protecting myself. It can mean: I trust you enough to be vulnerable. The same gesture, entirely different meanings.

Name the backs. The ones that hurt. The ones that freed you. The ones you still see when you close your eyes.
