Everyone needs a witness. Someone who sees you without the performance, without the filters, without the careful editing.

A confidante is not always a person. It might be a journal that never judges. A long walk where you talk to yourself. A voice note recorded at 2 a.m. that you delete by morning but needed to say out loud.

The point is not who or what it is. The point is that you have a place where you can be completely honest. Name that place. Honour it. It is keeping you whole.
