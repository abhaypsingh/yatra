the nine emotions

Two thousand years ago, the Indian sage Bharata wrote that all of human feeling flows from nine essences — the Navarasa.

Love, joy, compassion, anger, courage, fear, aversion, wonder, peace. Every day, some of these are alive in you. Most go unnamed.

Name them. Write one line for each. Over time, a mandala of your inner life will emerge — a map of who you are beneath the surface.

What you can name, you can understand. What you understand, you can hold gently.

Rasa रस

Rasa (Sanskrit: essence, flavor, emotion) is the aesthetic theory that all human emotional experience flows from nine fundamental essences. First articulated in the Natyashastra around the 2nd century BCE, the Navarasa remain one of the most elegant maps of the inner life ever created.

This is not a mood tracker. It is a practice of emotional literacy — the act of noticing what is alive in you, naming it honestly, and watching it change over time.
