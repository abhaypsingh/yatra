Gawāh

A gawah in a courtroom swears to tell the truth about what they saw. But outside the courtroom, most witnessing goes unsworn. You see a parent sacrifice silently. You watch a friend lose everything with dignity. You observe a stranger’s small act of kindness that no one else noticed. These moments were not yours, yet they entered you and rearranged something inside.

The witness carries a peculiar burden — the weight of someone else’s story lodged in your memory. You cannot tell it as your own, but you cannot forget it either. It sits inside you, shaping how you see the world.
