Everyone carries a shield. Humour. Silence. Anger. Perfection. The thing you hold up so the world cannot reach the tender places inside. Some shields you chose. Some chose you. Dhaal asks you to name what you carry — and wonder what would happen if you set it down.

Ḍhāl

A shield is not a weakness. It is proof that you have something worth protecting.

But every shield has a cost. The humour that deflects pain also deflects intimacy. The perfectionism that earns praise also earns exhaustion. The anger that keeps people away also keeps love away.

You do not have to drop your shields. But you should know you are carrying them. Because sometimes the bravest thing is not to fight harder, but to stand unguarded and let someone see you.
