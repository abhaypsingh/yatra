Chashmā

In the mountains of Kashmir and the dry plains of Rajasthan alike, a chashma is sacred. Water that finds its own way to the surface — unbidden, unexpected — is considered a gift. Nobody built it. Nobody planned it. It simply appeared because the pressure beneath the ground had nowhere else to go.

You have springs inside you. Reserves of patience, courage, or clarity that you never knew existed until a crisis cracked you open. They surfaced not because you searched for them but because you had no choice — and suddenly, there they were.

Name the springs. Honour the strength that found its own way up.
