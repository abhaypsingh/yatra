Water takes the shape of whatever holds it. A glass, a river, a cupped hand. You are like that too — one person with your parents, another with your friends, someone else when you are alone. Paani asks you to notice the shapes you take. Not to judge them, but to ask: which shape feels most like me?

Being like water is not weakness. It is intelligence. Water adapts, finds cracks, wears down stone, and nourishes everything it touches.

But water that has no container at all just spills and disappears. And water forced into too tight a shape loses its flow.

Notice the shapes you take with different people, in different places. Some shapes feel natural. Some feel forced. Name them — and you will begin to understand who you are when no one is holding you.
