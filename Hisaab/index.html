Hisāb

the account

Hisaab is the running ledger between people who love each other — favours given, slights received, sacrifices made, words left unsaid. It is the account that is never meant to balance but is always being tallied. Everyone keeps a hisaab, even those who say they don’t. Hisāb asks you to name the accounts between you and those you love — the invisible arithmetic of your closest relationships.

Hisāb

Every shopkeeper in India keeps a hisaab — a notebook with columns of credit and debit, names and amounts, promises and payments. But the most complex hisaab is not in any shop. It is between parent and child, between spouses, between old friends. Who sacrificed more. Who forgave less. Who showed up and who didn’t. The entries are never written down but they are never forgotten.

The tragedy of hisaab is that both sides believe their version. The parent thinks the ledger shows years of selflessness. The child thinks it shows years of control. Both are reading the same account and seeing different numbers. Love does not eliminate hisaab — it makes the arithmetic more complicated.

Name the accounts. Look at what the ledger actually says, not what you wish it said.
