Tanhā

The world is loud. It tells you to connect, to share, to never be alone. But some things can only be heard in silence. Some truths only arrive when there is no one else in the room.

Solitude is not loneliness. Loneliness is the absence of others. Solitude is the presence of yourself. It is the quiet that lets you listen to the voice underneath all the noise — your own.

Name the moments when being alone was not empty, but full. When the silence taught you something the noise never could.
