Vāris

The weight of inheritance is not only in what you receive but in what you must decide to keep. Every heir is also a curator — sorting through what was left behind, choosing what to carry forward and what to quietly set down. You inherit your father’s pride and must decide if it serves you. You inherit your mother’s anxiety and must decide if you will pass it on. The heir’s real work is not receiving but choosing.

You are a vaaris. You received habits, beliefs, patterns, and stories from those who came before. Some you kept because they served you. Some you kept because you didn’t know how to put them down. Some you left behind, and that leaving was its own form of courage.

Name the inheritances. They are the legacy you are still curating.
