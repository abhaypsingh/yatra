the jackfruit

A kathal is enormous, prickly on the outside, sticky and difficult to cut open, but inside it holds dozens of sweet, golden pods. Getting to the sweetness requires oil on your hands, patience, and a willingness to get messy. Kathal asks you to name the things in your life that were difficult to open but rich inside — the people, subjects, or experiences that rewarded those who persisted past the prickly exterior.

Kathal

Most people walk past the kathal because it looks impossible. Too big. Too spiky. Too sticky. But the ones who know — they oil their hands, take a sharp knife, and cut into the chaos. And what they find inside is unlike anything else: golden, aromatic, sweet beyond expectation. The difficulty is the gatekeeper, not the truth.

Your life has kathals. The relationships that required effort before they yielded intimacy. The skills that were frustrating before they became fluent. The truths that were uncomfortable before they became liberating.

Name the jackfruits. The sweetest things in life are not the easiest to reach.
