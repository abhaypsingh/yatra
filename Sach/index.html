There are truths you learn from others and truths you learn from experience. The second kind is harder and more valuable. Nobody can teach you what disappointment feels like. You have to learn it.

Then there are the truths you speak. Telling a friend something they do not want to hear. Admitting you were wrong. Saying I do not know. These truths are expensive. They cost you comfort. But they buy you something better: integrity.

Name your truths. The ones you learned, the ones you spoke, and the ones you are still working up the courage to say.
