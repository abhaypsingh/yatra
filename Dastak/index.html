the knock

A dastak is the sound that divides time into before and after. A knock at the door — sometimes it brings opportunity, sometimes catastrophe, sometimes a person you never expected to see again. The knock itself is neutral; what follows changes everything. Dastak asks you to recall the knocks in your life — the unexpected arrivals, the news at the threshold, the moments when fate announced itself with a simple sound.

Dastak

You were sitting in an ordinary evening. The tea was getting cold. The television was on. Then: a knock. And in the pause between hearing it and opening the door, your entire life hung in suspension — because you did not yet know whether what waited on the other side was a gift or a reckoning.

Some knocks brought people back from the dead — the friend who appeared after years of silence, the parent who returned after leaving. Others brought the dead closer — the officer at the door, the doctor in the hallway, the neighbour who could not meet your eyes. And some knocks were subtler: an idea that arrived uninvited, a chance encounter that rerouted your entire trajectory.

Record the knocks that mattered. Each one was an interruption that proved your life was never entirely under your control — and that sometimes, that was the point.
