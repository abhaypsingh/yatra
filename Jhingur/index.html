Jhingur

The cricket is never seen. It hides in the grass, behind a wall, inside a crack. But its song is inescapable — a single tiny creature filling the vast night with sound. It does not sing to be heard. It sings because that is its nature. And its persistence turns the silence into something inhabited.

There are jhingurs in every life — small, unseen presences whose steady voice became the background of your world. A grandmother’s muttered prayers. A neighbour’s radio. A friend who kept calling even when you stopped picking up. Small voices, vast silences, and the stubborn refusal to stop singing.

Name the crickets. They sang when no one else would.
