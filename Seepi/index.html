Sīpī

A shell is not the storm. It is what the storm left behind. The pearl inside the oyster did not form in calm waters. It formed around a grain of sand that should not have been there — an irritation, a wound, a thing that did not belong.

Your shells are the same. The empathy you have because you were once lonely. The patience you learned from waiting for something that never came. The quiet confidence that grew from failing and trying again.

Not every hard thing leaves a shell. But if you look carefully at the shore of your life, you will find them — small, luminous, yours.
