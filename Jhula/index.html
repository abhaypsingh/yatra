the swing

Some feelings swing — joy to sadness, confidence to doubt, closeness to distance. You are up one moment and down the next, and the motion itself is exhausting. Jhula asks you to name the swings. Not to stop them, but to notice the rhythm — and find your balance in the middle.

Jhūlā

A swing is not a pendulum. A pendulum is mechanical. A swing has a person on it — someone who feels the rush of rising and the drop of falling.

Your emotions swing. Your friendships swing. Your confidence swings. This is not a flaw. It is what it means to be alive and growing.

But if you can name the swing — if you can say “I am on the upswing” or “I am dropping now” — you gain something precious: the knowledge that it will swing back.
