Taubā

The Arabic root of taubah means “to return” — not to go backward, but to return to your true self. Repentance in this tradition is not punishment; it is homecoming. You strayed, you recognized the straying, and you came back. The act of return is honoured, not the act of having been perfect all along.

Real taubah happens in private. It is the 3 a.m. reckoning with yourself when the excuse you’ve been using finally crumbles. It is the morning you wake up and simply cannot continue the pattern. Not dramatic, not theatrical — just the quiet, enormous decision to stop. The world may never notice, but you are fundamentally reoriented.

Name your turnings. They are not evidence of failure — they are evidence that you are still awake to your own life.
