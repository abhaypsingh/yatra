There is a saying: lakeer ka fakeer — one who blindly follows the line drawn by others. Lines divide, lines connect, lines mark where something ends and something else begins. Lakeer asks you to name the lines in your life — the ones drawn for you and the ones you chose to cross.

Lakīr

Before you could think for yourself, lines were drawn for you. This is acceptable, that is not. These people are ours, those are not. This is your future, that is someone else’s.

Some lines protect you. The line between safety and danger. The line between kindness and cruelty. But some lines imprison you. The line between “boys do this” and “girls do that.” The line between your caste and theirs. The line between who you are and who they want you to be.

Name the lines. Then decide which ones to honour and which ones to erase.
