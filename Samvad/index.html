Samvad means dialogue — the meeting of two voices.

Most of what shapes us doesn’t happen in books or classrooms. It happens in conversations. A question your father asked at dinner. Something a friend said on a walk home. An argument that hurt but taught you something you needed to learn.

You don’t need to remember every word. Just the ones that mattered.
