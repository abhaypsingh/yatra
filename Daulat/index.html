Daulat

The word daulat once meant fortune in its widest sense — not just coins and land, but the total endowment of a life. A person rich in daulat might have little money but vast stores of knowledge, deep relationships, hard-won peace. The poorest-looking elder in the village might hold the greatest daulat of all.

We have narrowed wealth to numbers. But you know better. You know that some of your richest moments cost nothing, and some of your most expensive purchases left you poorer in spirit. Daulat is the accounting that matters — the ledger of what truly sustains you.

Take inventory. Count what you have that compound interest cannot touch and inflation cannot erode.
