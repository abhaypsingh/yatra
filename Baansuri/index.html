Bānsurī

the bamboo flute

A baansuri is a hollow piece of bamboo with holes burned into it. It makes music only because it is empty — because the breath passes through the hollow, shaped by the holes. The flute that is solid makes no sound. Bānsurī asks you to name the music you have made from your emptiness — the beauty that emerged from your hollows, losses, and openings.

Bānsurī

Krishna’s flute is the most beloved instrument in Indian mythology. But consider what it is: a stick of bamboo, hollowed out, with holes burned through it. Every hole is a wound. Every emptiness is a prerequisite. The baansuri teaches that music requires hollowness. A solid bamboo can be a staff or a weapon — but only the empty one can sing.

Your losses hollowed you. Your wounds burned holes into you. And through those hollows and holes, life’s breath now makes music that a solid person could never produce. Your emptiness is not your weakness — it is your instrument.

Name the flutes. They are the music that only your particular emptiness could make.
