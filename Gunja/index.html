Words have weight. Some land softly and disappear. Others hit a wall inside you and bounce back — again and again — for years.

The echo might be beautiful: a compliment that made you believe in yourself. Or it might be painful: a careless sentence that planted a doubt you still carry. Either way, the echo is telling you something about what matters to you.

Write down the words that still ring. Sometimes naming the echo is the first step to understanding it — or finally letting it go.
