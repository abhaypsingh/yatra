You felt the urge to react — to defend, to demand, to run, to fix. And six times, you chose to hold steady.

Patience is not weakness. It is the deepest kind of power — the power to let the water settle, and see clearly what is beneath.

Dhairya धैर्य

Dhairya means patience, steadfastness, fortitude. In the Bhagavad Gita, it is listed among the divine qualities — not as passive waiting, but as the active strength to remain composed when the world pushes you to break.

These six scenarios are drawn from the real tensions of growing up. The urge to react is natural. The choice to wait is extraordinary.
