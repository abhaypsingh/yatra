Sāyā

the shadow

A saaya is a shadow — the dark shape that proves something is standing in the light. It can be the comforting presence of a protector walking beside you, or the haunting outline of someone who is gone but whose shape still falls across your days. A shadow asks nothing and explains nothing; it simply follows. Sāyā asks you to name the shadows in your life — the presences, living or remembered, that moved when you moved and stayed when you stayed.

Whose shadow has followed you through your life?

Sāyā

When someone says “their saaya was over me,” they mean they were protected — shielded by a presence so constant it became invisible, like shade on a hot afternoon. You do not notice a shadow until it is gone. Then the sun hits you directly and you understand, for the first time, how much heat someone else had been absorbing on your behalf.

But shadows can also haunt. The outline of a parent in every authority figure you meet. The silhouette of a lost love in every doorway. Some shadows do not belong to the living at all — they belong to the dead, and they grow longer as the years pass, not shorter. To live with a saaya is to accept that you are never entirely alone, for better or for worse.

Name the shadows. Acknowledge who stood between you and the sun.
