Safe in your body. Safe to feel. Safe to be exactly who you are. Safe to say no. Safe to fail. Safe to be alone. These are not luxuries. They are rights.

If you have all six, protect them fiercely. If some are missing, that is not your fault, and it is not forever. You can build safety from the inside even when the outside is uncertain. And you can let people in to help you build it.

The Sanskrit word abhaya appears in every major tradition of India. It means: do not be afraid. Not because nothing can hurt you. But because you are more than anything that can.

Abhaya अभय

Abhaya means freedom from fear. In Hindu and Buddhist iconography, the abhaya mudra — the open palm raised outward — is the gesture of protection and reassurance. It says: you are safe here.

Psychological safety is the single strongest predictor of healthy development in children. It is not about removing all danger. It is about knowing that when danger comes, you will not face it alone.

Every child deserves to feel safe in these six ways. If any are missing, it is never too late to rebuild them.
