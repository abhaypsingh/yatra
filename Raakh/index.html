the ash

After something burns — a friendship, a belief, a version of yourself — there is ash. Ash is what remains when the fire is done. It is not nothing. It is the residue of transformation. Raakh asks you to name what has burned in your life and look at what remained. Not everything that ends is a loss.

Fire terrifies because it destroys. But fire also transforms. Ash is not absence. It is evidence that something lived, burned bright, and changed form.

The friendship that ended painfully left you with the knowledge of what loyalty actually means. The belief you lost left space for a truer one. The version of you that burned away made room for who you are becoming.

Sift through the ash. Not everything there is grief. Some of it is wisdom. Some of it is freedom. Name what burned. Then look carefully at what remained.
