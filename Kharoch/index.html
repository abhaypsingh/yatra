the scratch

A scratch is not a wound. It is not deep enough to bleed much or leave a real scar. But scratches accumulate. A dismissive comment. Being overlooked. A small unfairness no one else noticed. Alone, each one is nothing. Together, they sting. Kharoch asks you to name the scratches — the small, daily hurts that add up to something bigger than any single one.

Kharonch

Nobody takes a scratch seriously. “It is nothing,” they say. “Don’t be so sensitive.” But they do not live inside your skin. They do not know how many scratches are already there.

Being the last one picked. Being corrected in front of everyone. Being told your feelings are too much. Each one alone is survivable. But together they create a rawness that makes the next one unbearable.

You are not being dramatic. Scratches are real. Name them. When you see the collection, you will understand why you sometimes flinch at things others call small.
