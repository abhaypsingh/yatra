Fariyād

A fariyaad is not the same as a request. A request still has composure. A fariyaad has none. It is the voice that breaks in a courtroom, the cry at a shrine when medicine has failed, the phone call at three in the morning when silence has become unbearable. It comes from the place where self-sufficiency ends and raw human need begins.

There is no shame in a fariyaad, though it often feels like shame. To plead is to admit that you are not enough on your own — and that admission is not weakness. It is the most honest thing a person can do.

Name the pleas. Honour the moments you chose honesty over pride.
