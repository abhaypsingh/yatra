A rope can pull you to safety or tie you down. It connects climbers on a mountain and prisoners to a post. The same object, entirely different purposes. Rassi asks you to name the ropes in your life — the bonds that held you, the ties that saved you, and the ones that need to be cut.

Rassī

In childhood, a rassi is joy — skipping rope, tug of war, the clothesline where your school uniform dries in the sun. In life, a rassi is more complicated. Family ties. Social obligations. Emotional bonds. Each one a rope that connects you to something or someone.

Some ropes are lifelines. The friend who throws you a rope when you are drowning. The tradition that anchors you when everything else is shifting. But some ropes are chains in disguise. The guilt that ties you to a relationship you have outgrown.

Name the ropes. Hold the ones that save you. Consider cutting the ones that bind.
