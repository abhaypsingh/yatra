Mohur

the gold coin

A mohur was a gold coin of the Mughal era — rare, heavy, stamped with authority. Families kept mohurs hidden, never spending them, passing them down through generations. The mohur was not currency — it was legacy. Mohur asks you to name the rare treasures in your life that you refuse to spend — the values, memories, or gifts so precious that you hold them not for use but for meaning.

Mohur

The mohur was gold, but its value was never in the gold. It was in the keeping. A family that held a mohur for five generations was not rich because of one coin — it was rich because it had something it refused to sell. In a world that prices everything, the mohur was priceless precisely because it was never traded.

You have mohurs. The integrity you maintained when it would have been profitable to compromise. The relationship you protected when it was easier to let go. The standard you held when everyone around you had lowered theirs.

Name the gold coins. They are the things you are wealthy in because you refuse to spend them.
