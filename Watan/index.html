Watan

The word watan carries a specific grief in Urdu — the grief of distance from the land you love. Millions have carried this word across oceans, on trains during Partition, in aeroplanes to countries where the soil smells different and the birds sing in unfamiliar keys. Watan is not just where you were born. It is where your body remembers being at peace.

Your homelands may be many. The town where your mother grew up and took you every summer. The neighbourhood you left when the family moved. The country your ancestors walked away from so that you could have a different life. Even a room can be a watan — a kitchen, a rooftop, a corner of a garden where you felt you belonged completely.

Name the homelands. They are the geographies of your belonging.
