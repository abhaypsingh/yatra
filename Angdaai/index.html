Angḍāī

the stretch

When you wake up, sometimes your body stretches before your mind is ready. Arms reaching, back arching, a yawn that seems to come from somewhere deeper than sleep. Angdaai is the body remembering itself. It asks you to name the moments when you stretched — physically or otherwise — reaching for something just beyond your grasp.

Angḍāī

The angdaai is your body’s first act of courage each morning. Before you have decided anything, before you have checked your phone, your body reaches outward. It claims space. It says: I am here, I am alive, I need room.

You stretch in other ways too. When you try something new. When you say something honest. When you love someone even though it scares you. Every stretch is a small act of becoming larger than you were a moment ago.

Name the stretches. The ones that made you bigger. The ones that ached. The ones that felt like freedom.
