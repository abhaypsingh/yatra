Gaddhā

the pit

A gaddha is a hole in the ground — sometimes dug on purpose, sometimes the earth simply gives way. You fall before you see it. The descent is sudden. The climb out is slow. Gaddhā asks you to name the pits you have fallen into — the depressions, failures, or collapses that took you below ground level, and the slow work of climbing back to the surface.

Gaddhā

Nobody plans to fall into a gaddha. That is the nature of pits — they appear where you expect solid ground. One step and the earth gives way. You find yourself below the surface, looking up at a world that continues without you. The sky seems very far from a pit.

But pits teach. They teach that the ground is not always trustworthy. They teach the strength in your arms when you must pull yourself up with no ladder. They teach that the view from ground level, after you have been below it, is more beautiful than before you fell.

Name the pits. They are where you learned the strength of your own climb.
