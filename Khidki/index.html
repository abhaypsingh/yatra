Every window frames a different world. The window of your classroom shows one sky, the window of your bedroom another. What you see depends on where you stand. Khidki asks you to notice the views you look through — and wonder what someone else sees from theirs.

Khiḍkī

We all think we see the world as it is. But we see it through windows — our circumstances, our mood, our memory.

The window of privilege shows a different street than the window of struggle. The window of heartbreak colours the sunset differently than the window of falling in love.

You cannot remove all windows. But you can notice which one you are looking through. And sometimes, you can walk to a different one.
