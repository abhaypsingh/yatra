Waqt

the time

Time — the one resource that cannot be earned back. The hours that defined and the hours that were wasted. Waqt is not the clock on the wall but the weight of moments lived and lost. Waqt asks you to look at time not as a schedule but as a substance — thick with meaning in some seasons, thin and empty in others.

Waqt

Waqt is the great equalizer. No one, regardless of wealth or power, receives more than twenty-four hours in a day. And yet, some hours contain entire lifetimes — the hour your child was born, the hour you received a diagnosis, the hour you sat beside someone as they left this world. Other hours vanish without a trace, consumed by routine, distraction, or the slow erosion of waiting.

The cruelty of waqt is that you only understand its value after it has passed. The years spent in a job you hated reveal their cost only in retrospect. The summers with your parents that felt ordinary become priceless when those summers end. Waqt teaches through absence — you learn what an hour was worth only when you can never have it back.

Account for your time. Not to punish yourself for what was wasted, but to honour what was well spent and to spend what remains with open eyes.
