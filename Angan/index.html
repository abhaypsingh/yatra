Āngan

the courtyard

An angan is the open centre of an Indian home — roofless, surrounded by rooms on all sides. The sky enters here. Rain falls here. Children play here. Weddings happen here. It is the heart of the house, and it is open to the heavens. Āngan asks you to name the open centres of your life — the spaces in you or around you where sky meets ground, where the private and the infinite touch.

Āngan

The genius of the angan is that the house is built around emptiness. The most important part of the home is the part with no roof, no walls, no furniture — just open air. In the angan, you are inside your home and under the sky simultaneously. You are sheltered and exposed. Private and infinite.

You have angans within you. The openness you maintain despite the rooms of your life closing in. The vulnerability at the centre of your strength. The space you leave unbuilt because you know that the heart of anything must breathe.
