the cruelty

Sitam is cruelty with intention — not an accident, not collateral damage, but the deliberate choice to cause pain. It arrives from those you trusted or from strangers who wielded power without mercy. Sitam asks you to name the cruelties inflicted upon you — the acts that tested your belief in human goodness and left marks that required more than time to heal.

Sitam

The worst thing about cruelty is not the pain. Pain passes. The worst thing is the question it leaves behind: how could someone choose to do this? Sitam shatters the assumption that people are fundamentally kind. It introduces a darker knowledge — that some people are capable of inflicting suffering deliberately, and that sometimes those people are the ones who were supposed to protect you.

A parent who used silence as a weapon. A teacher who singled out the weakest child. An institution that ground its poorest members into dust while rewarding compliance. These sitam leave a residue that no apology fully dissolves — not because forgiveness is impossible, but because the knowledge cannot be unlearned.

Name the cruelties you survived. You are not defined by what was done to you, but what you did with the wound deserves to be witnessed.
