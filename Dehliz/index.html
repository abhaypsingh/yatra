Dehlīz

the corridor

A dehliz is the narrow passage between one room and another — not a room itself, but the space between rooms. You do not live in a dehliz; you pass through it. Yet it connects everything. Without the corridor, rooms are just isolated boxes. Dehlīz asks you to name the passages in your life — the transitional spaces between one phase and the next, where you were neither here nor there but moving.

Dehlīz

Nobody decorates the dehliz. It is the forgotten architecture of a house — the narrow space everyone passes through but no one claims. And yet it is the most important part of the house. Without it, you cannot get from the kitchen to the bedroom, from the public room to the private one. The corridor is what makes a house liveable.

Your dehlizes are the transition periods. The months between jobs. The years between who you were and who you became. The conversations that moved you from one understanding to another. They were uncomfortable — narrow, dim, purposeless-seeming. But they connected everything.

Name the corridors. They are how you got from room to room.
