Lehjā

Words can be chosen carefully, rehearsed, polished. But the lehja — the accent, the tone, the way the voice bends around a syllable — cannot be faked for long. It is the body’s truth leaking through the mouth’s composure. A person says “I’m fine” and the lehja says they are not. A person says “it doesn’t matter” and the lehja says it is the only thing that matters.

You have heard these tones. The accent of grief disguised as anger. The tone of love disguised as scolding. The careful flatness of someone who has rehearsed their calm. And you have your own lehja — the accent of your experience that colours every word you speak.

Name the tones. Listen again to what was really being said.
