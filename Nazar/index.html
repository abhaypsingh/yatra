The gaze — a look that carries meaning heavier than language, that sees through and into. A nazar can bless or wound, protect or expose. It is the unspoken verdict delivered by the eyes alone. Nazar asks you to recall the gazes that altered you — the looks of love, judgement, recognition, or dismissal that needed no words.

Nazar

Before language, there was the gaze. A mother looks at her newborn and something is transmitted that no sentence could carry. A teacher glances at a student and the student knows, without a word, whether they are believed in or written off. The nazar is the oldest and most honest form of communication — because the eyes have not yet learned the sophisticated lies that the tongue has mastered.

Some gazes land on you and you grow taller. Others land and you shrink. A lover’s nazar can make you feel like the only person in a crowded room. A parent’s disappointed nazar can make you feel alone in your own home. These gazes shape your sense of self more profoundly than any lecture or praise.

Remember the eyes that saw you. Record the gazes that you still feel on your skin years later.
