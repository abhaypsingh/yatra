Dukān

A dukaan is not a supermarket. It has a face. It has a name. It remembers what you bought last time. The kirana store uncle writes your purchases in a bahi khata and settles at month-end because trust is worth more than cash.

The bookshop where you discovered your first novel. The chai stall where you sat after school. The medical store at midnight during a family emergency. These are not retail transactions. They are relationships. They are community.

Name the shops. They are the small economies of love and trust that hold neighbourhoods together.
