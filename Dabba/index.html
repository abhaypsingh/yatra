Dabbā

The dabba is a love letter written in food. Someone woke before you, stood in the kitchen while you slept, and thought: what will they need today? The steel container that travels from home to school, from home to office, carries not just calories but care. It is nourishment packed in advance — love that travels.

Opening the dabba at noon is a small reunion with home. The smell of what was cooked at dawn reaches you hours later, in a different place, among different people. And for a moment, you are back in the kitchen where someone thought of you before you were even hungry.

Name the lunchboxes. They are the meals that carried a home inside them.
