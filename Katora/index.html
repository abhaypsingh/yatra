Katorā

the bowl

A katorā is the small metal bowl used for serving — daal in one, raita in another, chutney in a third. Each katora holds one thing, holds it well, and presents it alongside others on a thali. Alone it is incomplete; together with the others, it makes a whole meal. Katorā asks you to name the small, distinct offerings that were presented to you — each one holding something specific, each one essential to the wholeness of your life.

Katorā

The genius of the thali is that no single katora tries to be the whole meal. The daal does not pretend to be dessert. The pickle does not try to be rice. Each bowl knows its purpose and holds its offering with confidence. The wholeness emerges not from any one bowl but from the arrangement of all of them together on the plate.

Your katoras were the specific, bounded gifts. A friend who gave you laughter but not advice. A parent who gave you security but not words. A teacher who gave you rigour but not warmth. No single person or experience could be everything. But together, the bowls made a complete meal.
