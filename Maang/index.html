the demand

Everyone demands something from you. Your parents demand marks. Your friends demand loyalty. Teachers demand attention. Society demands conformity. And somewhere in the noise, you have demands of your own — for freedom, for understanding, for the right to be imperfect. Maang asks you to name the demands — and wonder which ones you actually owe.

Māng

A demand is different from a request. A request can be declined. A demand comes with consequences.

Some demands are fair — your parents demanding basic respect, your school demanding that you try. Some are unfair — the demand to always be happy, to never fail, to have your life figured out at seventeen.

And your own demands matter too. You demand to be heard. You demand space to make mistakes. You demand the right to grow at your own pace. Name all the demands. Then decide which ones to honour and which ones to quietly set aside.
