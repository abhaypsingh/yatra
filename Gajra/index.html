Gajrā

the flower garland

A gajra is a string of jasmine flowers woven into the hair. It fills the room with fragrance. It turns the ordinary into the special. But jasmine wilts by evening — the beauty is temporary by design. Gajra asks you to name the fleeting beauties — the things that were glorious precisely because they did not last.

Gajrā

The gajra vendor sits on the street corner in the evening. The flowers are fresh. By tomorrow they will be brown. This is not a flaw — it is the point. Jasmine gives everything in a few hours: all its beauty, all its fragrance, all its sweetness. Then it is done.

The best moments of your life are gajras. A festival night. A conversation that made everything clear. A hug from someone who is no longer here. They did not last. But the fragrance lingers in your memory long after the flowers have dried.

Name the gajras. They are proof that some things do not need to last forever to matter forever.
