Friendship is not a destination.

It is a practice. A daily choice to show up for another person — not the perfect version of them, but the real one. And to let them show up for the real version of you.

You will lose friends. You will outgrow friends. You will be hurt by friends. And you will find the ones who stay. The ones who see you at your worst and choose you anyway. Those friendships will be worth every one you lost along the way.

You are not too much. You are not too little. You are exactly the kind of friend someone out there is looking for.

Sakhī सखी

Sakhī means friend, companion, confidante. In Sanskrit poetry, the sakhī is the trusted friend to whom you tell everything — the one who knows your heart better than you do.

Research shows that the quality of adolescent friendships is one of the strongest predictors of adult wellbeing — stronger than academic achievement, stronger than family income. The friends you choose now shape who you become.

Every friendship teaches you something. Even the ones that end.
