Khwāb

A khwab operates in two realms. There is the dream that visits at night — unbidden, strange, sometimes prophetic. And there is the dream you construct with open eyes — the ambition, the vision, the imagined future that makes the difficult present bearable. Both are real. Both shape your actions even when you forget them by morning.

Some dreams are fulfilled and feel smaller than imagined. Others are never realized but remain the most important stories of your inner life. Your abandoned dreams tell as much about you as your achieved ones — perhaps more, because they reveal what you wanted before the world told you what to want.

Record your dreams — the nocturnal visitations and the waking blueprints alike. They are the architecture of your longing.
