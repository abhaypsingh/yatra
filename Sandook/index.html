Sandūq

the trunk

Every family has a sandook. A heavy metal trunk pushed under a bed or placed on top of an almirah. Inside it: old sarees, documents, jewellery, letters, things too important to display and too precious to throw away. Sandook asks you to name the trunks — the things you have locked away because they matter too much.

Sandūq

The sandook is the family vault. It smells of naphthalene and secrets. Your grandmother’s wedding saree is in there. Your father’s first pay slip. A photograph of someone no one talks about anymore.

You have sandooks too. Memories you have locked away. Dreams you stored “for later.” Feelings you packed tight and pushed under the bed of your consciousness. They are still there. They are waiting.

Name the trunks. Some need to stay locked. Some are ready to be opened.
