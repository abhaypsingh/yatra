Chabīl

Unconditional generosity is the purest form of human expression. The chabeel stands at the roadside in the killing heat, and anyone may drink. Rich, poor, known, unknown — the sweet water does not discriminate. The person who sets it up does not ask your name. They do not expect gratitude. They offer because the heat is real and the water is sweet and that is enough.

You have tasted chabeel. The anonymous kindness of a stranger. The help given without a ledger. The love offered without terms and conditions. These offerings cooled you in ways that transactional kindness never could.

Name the offerings. They are the proof that generosity without condition still exists.
