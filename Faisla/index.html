Faislā

Life is punctuated by faislas — verdicts that arrive from outside or from within and divide time into before and after. A doctor delivers a diagnosis. A court issues a ruling. A parent says “enough.” You look in the mirror one morning and decide you cannot continue as you have been. Each faisla closes a door with a sound that echoes long after.

The hardest faislas are the ones you deliver on yourself. To declare that a relationship is over, that a dream must be abandoned, that a version of yourself has expired — these self-verdicts require a brutal honesty that no external judge can match. They are also the most freeing, because no one can appeal a judgement you pass on your own life.

Name the verdicts. The ones that were delivered upon you and the ones you had the courage to deliver yourself. They are the hinges on which your life turned.
