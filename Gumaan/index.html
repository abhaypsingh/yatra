Gumān

Gumaan is a double-edged inheritance. It is the pride that made your grandfather walk tall when the world told him to bow. It is also the pride that made him refuse help when he desperately needed it. Gumaan gives you spine, but it can also make you rigid. It gives you standards, but it can also make you lonely.

The hardest question about pride is not whether you have it — everyone does — but whether you can see it clearly. The gumaan that you are always right prevents you from learning. The gumaan that you deserve dignity keeps you from accepting cruelty. Same word, vastly different consequences.

Look at your prides honestly. Name the ones that carried you and the ones that kept you from being carried when you needed it.
