Machhlī

The fish-in-water paradox is the oldest trick of existence: what surrounds you completely becomes invisible. You cannot see the culture you were raised in while you are still inside it. You cannot feel the pressure of the atmosphere because you have never known its absence. Only distance — a move, a loss, a rupture — reveals what was always there.

You have been the fish. Swimming in assumptions about how families work, what success means, who deserves what. These waters shaped every movement you made, and you did not know they were there until something pulled you out.

Name the waters. They are the invisible mediums that made you who you are.
