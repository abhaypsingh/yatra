Prison is not always a cell — it can be a job, a relationship, a belief, a body, an expectation. Qaid is any confinement where the walls are real even if they are invisible. Qaid asks you to name the imprisonments you have known and what you did with the time inside.

Qaid

Some of the greatest literature in Urdu and Hindi was written in prison — because confinement, paradoxically, can concentrate the mind. When the body cannot move, the spirit sometimes travels farther than it ever did in freedom. The qaid is not just the walls. It is also what happens inside them.

Your imprisonments may not have had iron bars, but they were real. A marriage you could not leave. A debt you could not escape. A grief you could not move past. Inside each confinement, you did something — you endured, you thought, you planned, you grew, or you simply survived. That is not nothing.

Name the cells. And name what you built inside them.
