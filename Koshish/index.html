Nobody remembers the safe choices. They remember the attempts — the time you raised your hand when you were not sure of the answer, the time you apologised even though it was terrifying, the time you tried something new and failed and tried again.

An attempt is not measured by its outcome. It is measured by the courage it took to begin. Every attempt, successful or not, is proof that you are willing to risk being wrong in order to grow.

Write down your attempts. The ones that worked and the ones that did not. They all count.
