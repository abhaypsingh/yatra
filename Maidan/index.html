Maidān

the open field

A maidan is an open field — no walls, no fences, no marked paths. Children play cricket on it. Kites are flown there. Fairs set up and leave. It is the space of possibility: undefined, unowned, open to whatever arrives. Maidān asks you to name the open fields of your life — the unstructured spaces where anything could happen.

Maidān

The maidan has no purpose — and that is its purpose. It is not a garden, not a park, not a playground. It is simply open ground. And because it is undefined, it can become anything. A cricket pitch in the morning, a kite-flying ground in the afternoon, a fair in the evening, and an empty stillness at night.

Life needs maidans — stretches of time or space with no agenda. The gap year. The unplanned afternoon. The friendship with no obligation. These open fields are where possibility lives, because nothing has been decided yet.

Name your maidans. They are the spaces where you discovered what you wanted before anyone told you what to want.
