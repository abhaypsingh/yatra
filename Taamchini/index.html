Tāmchīnī

the enamelware

Taamchini is enamelware — the blue and white chipped cups and plates of every Indian kitchen. They dent but do not break. They chip but keep serving. They are not beautiful by design but beautiful by use. Taamchini asks you to name the chipped but cherished things — the imperfect vessels that have served you faithfully.

Every Indian home has that one taamchini cup. The enamel is chipped at the rim. The colour has faded. A newer, shinier cup sits right next to it. But the chai tastes right only in the old one.

The best things in your life might be taamchini. The friendship that has weathered fights — chipped but holding. The family that is dented by disagreements but still shows up. The self-belief that has been knocked around but refuses to shatter.
