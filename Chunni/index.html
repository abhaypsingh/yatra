Chunnī

Everyone wears veils. Not just the cloth kind. The polite smile when you are furious. The “I’m fine” when you are drowning. The confident walk when your knees are shaking. These are all chunnis.

Some veils protect you. The composure you maintain in a hostile room. The strength you project when you are scared. But some veils suffocate you. The performance of being someone you are not. The hiding of what you truly feel because the world is not ready for your truth.

Name the veils. Decide which ones are shields and which ones are cages.
