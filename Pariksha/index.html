Parīkshā

Parīkshā means test, trial, examination. But the tests that matter most have no grade and no teacher.

You could have lied, but you told the truth. You could have stayed silent, but you spoke up. You could have followed the crowd, but you walked away. You could have been cruel, but you chose kindness.

These are the tests that build who you are. Record them. Not to boast — to remember what you’re made of.
