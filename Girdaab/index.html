Girdāb

A whirlpool is not an enemy you can face. It has no body, no intention — only motion. It is a pattern that feeds on itself, drawing everything nearby into tighter and tighter circles. The harder you fight it directly, the more energy it takes from you. This is what makes certain life situations so devastating: they do not attack, they absorb.

Debt spirals. Grief cycles. Arguments that restart with the same words every time. Addictions that promise relief and deliver deeper need. These are girdaab — whirlpools that look like stillness from the outside but are violent underneath.

Name the ones that caught you. Not to relive the spinning, but to mark the moment you found your way out — or to acknowledge you are still searching for the edge.
