Zarūrat asks you to recall the times when necessity taught you what was essential — when want was removed and only need remained, and you discovered what you could and could not live without.

Necessity is the great editor. It takes the sprawling manuscript of your desires and cuts it down to a single sentence: what do you need right now to survive? In that brutal editing, profound clarity emerges. The things you thought you needed — the status, the comfort, the approval — fall away like dead leaves, and what remains is the trunk: food, shelter, one person who cares, the will to continue.

You have been edited by zaroorat. The time money ran out and you learned who your real friends were. The crisis that revealed which relationship was load-bearing and which was decorative. The illness that taught you the difference between wanting to live and choosing to live — actively, deliberately, one morning at a time.

Record the necessities that clarified your life. They were harsh teachers, but they showed you a truth that comfort never could.
