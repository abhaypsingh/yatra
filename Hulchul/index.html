Hulchul

Hulchul is necessary disruption. Things that have been still too long become stuck — relationships, habits, arrangements that no one questions because questioning would cause a commotion. And then the commotion arrives anyway. A fight. A move. A loss. A decision that upends everything. While it lasts, hulchul feels like destruction. But afterward, when the dust settles, you often find that what remains is truer than what was there before.

A good shaking loosens what needed to fall. The books that were stacked wrong find new shelves. The words that were never spoken are finally said. The life that was arranged by someone else is rearranged by you.
