Supnā

In Punjabi and Hindi, supna carries a tenderness that “dream” does not quite capture. A supna is fragile. It can shatter at the alarm clock. It can haunt you all day with a feeling you cannot name.

You have two kinds of supnas. The ones that come at night — strange, symbolic, sometimes terrifying. And the ones you carry during the day — the vision of who you could be, the life you imagine, the hope that pulls you forward.

Name them both. The night dreams teach you what your unconscious knows. The day dreams teach you what your heart wants.
