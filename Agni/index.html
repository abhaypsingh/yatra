Agni अग्नि

Agni (Sanskrit: fire) is among the most important Vedic deities — the god of fire, the messenger between humans and the divine. In Vedic ritual, offerings are placed into fire not to destroy them, but to transform them — to carry them from the material world into something beyond.

This experience applies that principle to the words we carry. The letters we write here are not destroyed. They are released. The act of writing makes the invisible visible. The act of burning transforms weight into light.

Your letters are never stored or transmitted. Only a trace remains in the ashes — the name of the recipient, the date, and a single line you choose to keep. Everything else is given to the fire.

The fire is rendered in real-time with particle physics. Each ember that rises was once a word. Every session is unique. Nothing is permanent. That is the point.
