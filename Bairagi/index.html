the renunciant

A bairagi is one who has let go — not from weakness, but from understanding. They leave behind what others cling to, not because it has no value, but because holding it prevents something larger. Bairāgī asks you to name what you have consciously renounced — the ambitions, attachments, or identities you walked away from so that your hands would be free for something else.

Bairāgī

The bairagi is often pitied by the householder: look at what they gave up. But the bairagi sees it differently: look at what I gained by having empty hands. Renunciation is not loss. It is strategic emptiness. It is the understanding that you cannot hold the new until you release the old.

You have been a bairagi. The promotion you declined because it would cost your evenings. The opinion you stopped defending because peace was worth more than being right. The identity you shed because it no longer fit who you were becoming.

Name the renunciations. They are the things you put down so you could pick up something better.
