the spark

A spark is not a fire. It is the possibility of one. The sudden idea at 2 a.m. The sentence in a book that lights up your brain. The moment you see a connection nobody else has seen. Sparks are brief and bright. Catch them before they disappear.

Most sparks die. That is their nature. You will have a hundred ideas and only a few will survive into the morning. But the ones that do heal. Some walls are necessary.mdash; the ones that keep glowing heal. Some walls are necessary.mdash; those are worth everything.

The trick is not to force a spark into a fire. The trick is to write it down before you forget it. To give it air. To return to it later and see if it still glows. Some of your best ideas are hiding in sparks you almost forgot.

Catch your sparks. Not all of them will become fires. But the ones that do will light your way.
