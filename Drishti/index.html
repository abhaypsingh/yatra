The Mirror

Six moments. Twelve perspectives. Every time, the story you saw first felt complete — until the other side revealed what you couldn't see.

Now turn the lens on your own life.

Think of someone you've judged, dismissed, or stopped listening to.

What is the side of their story you have never let yourself see?

Drishti दृष्टि

Drishti (Sanskrit: sight, vision, perspective) refers in yogic tradition to the focused gaze — the deliberate direction of one's seeing. In a broader sense, it is the capacity to see beyond the surface of things.

We all carry a single perspective. It feels like the whole truth. It never is. The person you find hardest to understand is the one whose drishti you have never tried to enter.

These six moments are composites drawn from real patterns in human relationships. None are fictional in the way that matters — every one of them is happening right now, somewhere, between two people who love each other and cannot see each other clearly.
