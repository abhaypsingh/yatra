You have spoken millions of words in your lifetime. Most dissolved instantly, carrying no more weight than breath. But a handful of them landed with the force of a hammer — on someone else, or on you. “I’m proud of you.” “We need to talk.” “I don’t love you anymore.” “You were right.” These alfaaz did not merely communicate — they acted. They rearranged reality.

Words spoken to you in childhood may still govern your adult behaviour. A teacher’s offhand remark can shape a career. A parent’s repeated phrase becomes the voice inside your head. Alfaaz are not ephemeral — they are the most durable material in human experience, outlasting stone and steel in the architecture of a person’s inner life.
