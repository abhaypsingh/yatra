Ūṇṭh

The camel does not complain about the desert. It was built for the desert. Its body stores what it needs, its feet are made for sand, and its pace is steady rather than fast. The camel teaches that endurance is not about ignoring hardship — it is about being prepared for it, carrying your reserves within, and trusting the direction even when the landscape offers nothing.

You have crossed deserts too. Stretches of life where there was no shade, no oasis, no encouragement. You kept walking because something inside you — stored long before the journey began — sustained you. A parent’s faith. A stubborn hope. A promise you made to yourself.

Name the crossings. They are the proof that you carried enough within you to survive what was barren outside.
