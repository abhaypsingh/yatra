Faqīr

A fakeer walks through the world owning almost nothing, yet the tradition insists the fakeer is the richest person alive. This is not romantic nonsense. It is the recognition that attachment is weight, and weight is suffering. The fakeer has done the terrifying arithmetic: calculated what things truly cost in worry, maintenance, and fear of loss — and decided the price is too high.

You do not need to renounce the world to understand this. You have already experienced small fakeeri — the relief of quitting something that consumed you, the clarity after ending a relationship that drained you, the peace of admitting you do not need what you once desperately wanted.

Name what you gave up. Honour the emptiness that turned out to be fullness.
