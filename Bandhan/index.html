the bond

A bond can be a blessing or a burden — the thread tied around a wrist in love, or the chain that will not let you leave. Every significant relationship is a bandhan: something that ties two lives together, for better or for worse, through choice or through circumstance. Bandhan asks you to name the bonds that have shaped your life — the ties that held firm and the ones that finally broke.

Bandhan

On Raksha Bandhan, a sister ties a thread around her brother’s wrist. It is thin enough to snap with one pull, yet it represents a bond that is supposed to outlast everything. That is the paradox of bandhan — the strongest bonds are often made of the most fragile material. A promise. A shared memory. A wordless understanding. They hold not because they are unbreakable, but because both people choose not to break them.

Your life is a web of bandhans. The bond with a parent that shifted as you grew — from dependence to distance to something harder to name. The friendship forged in a difficult season that became the most honest relationship you have. The obligation you resented but fulfilled because the bond was older than your objection. Some of these threads still hold. Some have frayed. Some were cut.

Name the bonds. They are the invisible threads that give your life its shape.
