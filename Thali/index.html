the plate

Life serves you a plate — and you do not get to choose everything on it. The family you were born into. The body you were given. The losses that arrived without warning. But also the unexpected joys. Thali asks you to look at what has been placed before you — the bitter and the sweet, the chosen and the given.

Thālī

An Indian thali holds many things at once — sweet, sour, bitter, salty. You do not eat them separately. They are all part of one meal.

Your life is the same. The difficult teacher who made you tougher. The heartbreak that taught you tenderness. The privilege you did not earn and the struggle you did not deserve.

You did not choose everything on your plate. But you can choose how you receive it. Name what has been served — and taste it honestly.
