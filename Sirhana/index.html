Sirhānā

The sirhana knows your secrets. It is the last thing you touch before sleep and the first thing you feel when you wake. It has absorbed your tears, your fever, your restless turning.

But a sirhana is not always a pillow. Sometimes it is a shoulder. Sometimes it is the ground. Sometimes it is the crook of your own arm when there is no one else. The quality of your rest tells you something about the quality of your life.

Name the headrests. The soft ones and the hard ones. The ones you chose and the ones that were all you had.
