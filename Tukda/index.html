the fragment

Not everything in your life is whole. There are fragments — half-finished conversations, friendships that ended too soon, memories that come in flashes. You carry these pieces without knowing why. Tukda asks you to name the fragments. The things that are broken or incomplete but still matter. Not everything needs to be whole to be important.

Ṭukḍā

We are taught to value completeness. Finish the task. Complete the course. Reach the goal. But life is full of things that break, that stop mid-sentence, that never get their ending.

A friendship that faded. A version of yourself you outgrew. A place you left before you were ready. These fragments are not failures. They are proof that you lived.

Hold them gently. Not every piece needs to be glued back together. Sometimes a fragment is enough.
