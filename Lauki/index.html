Laukī

the gourd

Laukī is the bottle gourd — the most unglamorous vegetable in the Indian kitchen. No child asks for it. No restaurant features it. It is watery, bland, and perpetually prescribed by doctors and grandmothers for anyone who is unwell. And yet — laukī heals. It cools the body, settles the stomach, and nourishes without demanding gratitude. Laukī asks you to name the bland nourishments that healed you — the unglamorous, unexciting remedies that worked precisely because they demanded nothing from you in return.
