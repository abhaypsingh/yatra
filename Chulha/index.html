The chulha is the heart of the home. It is where raw things become nourishing, where fire transforms grain into food, where smoke carries the smell of belonging. Every family has a chulha — a place where things are cooked slowly, with patience. Chūlhā

Long before gas stoves and induction cookers, there was the chulha. Mud and brick, shaped by hand, fed by wood and dung cakes. The woman of the house would sit before it every morning and evening, coaxing fire into food.

The chulha is where stories were told. Where a mother’s tears fell unseen into the dough. Where the smell of home was born. Every family’s food tastes different because every chulha carries a different history.
