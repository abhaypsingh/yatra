Chaukhaṭ

A doorframe is the most underappreciated part of a house. It holds the weight of the wall. It marks the boundary between rooms. And every meaningful transition in your life passes through one.

You stand at doorframes constantly — between being a child and not being one anymore. Between the safety of what you know and the terror of what you might become. Between saying the thing and holding it in.

You do not have to rush through. Stand in the doorframe. Look both ways. The threshold is not wasted time — it is where courage gathers itself.
