Āṅgan

the courtyard

In old Indian homes, the aangan is the open courtyard at the centre — where the sky meets the house, where family gathers, where children play. Think of the spaces in your life that feel like an aangan — the places where everything comes together, where you feel most at home. They do not have to be rooms. They can be moments, rituals, or people.

Āṅgan

The aangan is not a room. It is an opening. A place where the inside of the home meets the open sky.

In your life, the aangan might be the dinner table where stories are told. Or the car ride where you and your parent finally talk. Or the group chat where your friends are just themselves.

Every life needs a courtyard — a space where you do not perform, where you simply are. Name yours. Protect them.
