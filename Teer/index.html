An arrow flies in only one direction. Once released, it cannot be called back. Words are like that. A comment that pierced your heart. A sentence you aimed at someone in anger. A truth someone shot at you that you were not ready to catch. Teer asks you to name the arrows — the ones that hit you, and the ones you fired.

Tīr

An arrow is precise. It does not bruise like a punch. It pierces. And some words are exactly like that — they pass through your defences and lodge somewhere deep.

“You will never be as good as your sister.” “I expected more from you.” “You are just like your father.” These arrows may have been shot years ago, and they are still in you.

But you have fired arrows too. Words aimed to hurt, truths used as weapons. Name them all. The ones you carry and the ones you sent. Arrows teach us the weight of words.
