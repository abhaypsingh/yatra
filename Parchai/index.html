Parchāī

the shadow-self

Your parchai follows you everywhere but you can never touch it. It stretches when the light is low and shrinks when the sun is overhead. It is you and not-you at the same time. Parchai asks you to name the shadow-selves — the versions of you that follow you, the ones others see that you cannot, the dark outlines of who you might have been.

Parchāī

In folklore, your parchai can be stolen, trapped, or separated from you. This is metaphorically true. Parts of you get left behind in places. The child you were still exists as a shadow behind the adult you became. The person others think you are walks ahead of you like a parchai you cannot catch.

Sometimes your shadow is larger than you — when others overestimate you. Sometimes it is smaller — when you are diminished by circumstance. The shadow is always a distortion. But it is always connected to something real.

Name the shadows. Understanding them is understanding the light that casts them.
