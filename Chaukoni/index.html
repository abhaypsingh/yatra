Chaukonī

the square

Chaukonī means square — four equal sides, four right angles, everything measured and balanced. The square is the shape of stability: a room, a courtyard, a plot of land. It says: here are your boundaries, and within them, you are safe. Chaukonī asks you to name the four-cornered boundaries of your life — the structures, rules, and frames that gave you shape when you might otherwise have spread formlessly in every direction.

Chaukonī

A square is the most democratic shape: all sides equal, all angles the same. It does not tilt or lean. Within a square, you know exactly where you stand because the boundaries are clear and consistent. This is why rooms are square, why plots are square, why the chessboard is a grid of squares — clarity requires edges.

Your squares were the structures that made life navigable. The schedule that gave chaos a frame. The values that said “this far and no further.” The commitment that made a hundred daily decisions unnecessary because one big decision had already been made. Not every boundary is a cage. Some are the reason the garden grows.

Name the squares. The shape of your freedom was often drawn by the walls you chose to keep.
