the branch

A daali is a branch — it grows from the trunk but reaches in its own direction. It carries its own leaves, its own fruit, its own weight. Yet it is never separate from the tree. Every branch is both departure and belonging. Dālī asks you to name the branches that grew from your trunk — the directions your life took that were uniquely yours but rooted in who raised you.

No two branches of a tree grow in the same direction. Each one finds its own path to the light. But cut any branch and you will find the same rings as the trunk — the same years, the same droughts, the same seasons of rain. A branch is independence and inheritance at once.

Your life has daalis — the parts of you that grew away from your family’s expectations, your community’s norms, your own earlier plans. Yet when you look closely, the grain is the same. You are your own branch, but you carry the tree’s history.

Name the branches. They are how you reach for your own light.
