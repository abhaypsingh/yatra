It is not grief exactly; it is the residue of grief, the stain it leaves behind. Kasak asks you to name the aches that linger — the pains that outlived their cause.

Kasak

A kasak is what remains after the story is over. The film has ended, the lights have come on, the theatre is empty — but something still sits in your chest. It is not the memory of the event but the feeling the event left behind, like the impression a heavy object leaves on a cushion long after it has been removed. The shape remains even when the weight is gone.

You carry kasaks. The sting of a rejection you have long since moved past. The ache of a friendship that faded without a fight. The pang when a song plays that belonged to a time you cannot return to. These are not open wounds — they are closed ones that still throb when the weather changes.

Name the aches. Acknowledging them is not reopening the wound — it is honouring the scar.
