Kshama

forgiveness

Forgiveness is not saying what happened was okay.

It is deciding that you no longer want to carry the weight of it.

You are not doing this for them. You are doing this for you.

What happened?

Write what hurt you. Not to relive it, but to look at it clearly — one last time.

Feel the weight

How heavy does this feel right now?

What do you wish had happened instead?

Not what you think you should say. What you actually wish.

The choice

You can keep carrying this. Or you can set it down — not because it did not matter, but because you matter more than the weight.

Kshama क्षमा

Kshama means forgiveness, patience, endurance. It appears in ancient texts as one of the highest virtues — not weakness, but a form of strength that comes from choosing freedom over resentment.

Forgiveness research in psychology shows that the act of forgiving primarily benefits the person who forgives. It reduces anxiety, lowers blood pressure, and restores a sense of personal power.

You do not have to forgive today. You do not have to forgive ever. But if you choose to, it is a gift you give yourself.
