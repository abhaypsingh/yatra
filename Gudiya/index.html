Guḍiyā

the doll

A doll is made by someone else’s hands. Dressed how they choose. Posed how they like. Smiled when they want it to smile. Parts of you have been shaped the same way — by parents, by culture, by expectation. Gudiya asks you to name the parts of yourself that were designed by others — and the parts that are finally, unmistakably yours.

Guḍiyā

Before you could speak, someone was already deciding who you would be. The good child. The smart one. The quiet one. The one who would fulfil a dream that was not yours to begin with.

Not all of this shaping was harmful. Some of it gave you structure, values, direction. But some of it dressed you in clothes that never fit.

Growing up is the slow, brave act of picking up the scissors and altering the outfit yourself. Name what was designed for you. Then decide what to keep, what to alter, and what to discard entirely.
