Chilman

the curtain

A chilman is a curtain hung across a doorway — not a door, not a wall, but a soft boundary. You can hear through it, see shadows through it, push through it at any time. It divides without separating. Chilman asks you to name the soft boundaries in your life — the divisions that exist between you and others that are more fabric than brick, more suggestion than enforcement.

What soft boundaries exist in your life?

Chilman

A chilman is the most honest kind of boundary. It says: I am here, but I am not a wall. You can cross me. But please acknowledge that I exist. In old Indian homes, rooms were not always separated by doors — a curtain was enough. It gave the illusion of privacy while maintaining the reality of connection.

Your relationships have chilmans. The topics you don’t discuss but could. The emotions you sense through the fabric of someone’s composure. The boundaries that are real but permeable — respected not because they are strong, but because they are agreed upon.

Name the curtains. They are the gentle architecture of your relationships.
