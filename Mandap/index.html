Maṇḍap

the canopy

A mandap is the temporary canopy erected for a wedding or ceremony — four pillars, a roof of flowers and cloth, and a sacred fire at the centre. It lasts only as long as the ceremony, then it is dismantled. But what happens beneath it lasts a lifetime. Maṇḍap asks you to name the temporary sacred spaces in your life — the canopies under which permanent commitments were made.

Maṇḍap

The mandap is a paradox: a temporary structure for a permanent act. It is built in hours and dismantled in minutes, yet the vows taken beneath it are meant to last seven lifetimes. The flowers wilt. The cloth is folded and returned. The fire is ashes by morning. But the couple who walked around that fire are bound beyond the structure’s lifetime.

Your mandaps were the brief, sacred containers. The interview room where your career was decided. The hospital bed where your child was born. The park bench where a friendship became lifelong. Temporary spaces, permanent transformations.
