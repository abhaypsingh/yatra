Kangan

the bangle

A bangle is a circle you wear on your wrist. It is decoration and tradition, beauty and identity. But a bangle is also a circle that does not open — you must squeeze your hand to put it on, enduring a moment of pain for the beauty that follows. Kangan asks you to name the bangles — the beautiful things that required pain to wear.

Kangan

The sound of bangles is the sound of home. A mother cooking. A bride walking. A grandmother’s hands clapping at a song. The kangan carries music in its very existence.

But think about what it means to wear a circle that does not open. Every tradition, every role, every identity is a bangle. Some jingle with joy. Some are too tight. Some you wish you could take off but cannot because they mean too much to someone you love.

Name the bangles. The ones that make music and the ones that leave marks on your wrist.
