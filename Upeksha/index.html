Upekṣā उपेक्षा

Upekṣā is one of the four sublime states in Buddhist philosophy. It means equanimity — the ability to observe what arises without being swept away by it.

It is not indifference. It is not detachment. It is the deepest form of engagement: seeing clearly because you are not blinded by your first reaction.

Research in neuroscience shows that the pause between stimulus and response can be trained. Each time you practice it, the neural pathways for impulse control literally grow stronger.
