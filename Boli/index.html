Language is not just words. It is belonging. Every group you are part of has its own tongue — its own shortcuts, its own silences that mean something.

Your mother’s raised eyebrow that replaces an entire lecture. The one word between you and your best friend that makes you both laugh. The dialect your grandparents speak that you understand but cannot quite reply in.

These are not just communication. They are identity. Every tongue you carry is a world you belong to. Name them. They are more yours than any language a school can teach you.
