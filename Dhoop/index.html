Walls are not always bad. The wall around a garden keeps the deer out. The wall around your heart after a betrayal keeps you safe while you heal. Some walls are necessary.

But some walls outlast their purpose. The silence that protected you during a fight becomes a barrier to repair. The shyness that once kept you safe becomes a prison that keeps everyone out. The trick is knowing which walls to keep and which to take down, brick by brick.

Name your walls. Ask each one: are you protecting me, or are you trapping me?
