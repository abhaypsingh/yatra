Kachnar

Some beauty can only appear in barren seasons. The kachnar knows this — it waits for every other tree to shed its leaves, and only then does it open its flowers. As though colour requires emptiness. As though the bloom needs the bare branch to mean anything at all. There is a lesson in this: do not judge a season by its barrenness. It may be preparing to bloom.

You have seen kachnar moments. Times when nothing was growing, when life felt leafless and cold, and then — unexpectedly — something beautiful appeared. A person. An idea. A turning point that could only have arrived in that particular emptiness.

Name the blooms. They are proof that beauty has its own calendar.
