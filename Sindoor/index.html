the vermillion

Sindoor is a line of red in the parting of the hair. It declares: I belong to someone. I have been claimed. I have chosen. It is intimate and public at the same time. Sindoor asks you to name the marks of belonging — the visible and invisible signs that declare you are part of something larger than yourself.

Sindūr

Sindoor is the colour of auspiciousness, of commitment, of fire. It sits in the most visible place — the parting of the hair — and announces to the world: I am not alone. I am connected. I have roots.

You wear sindoors even if you never touch the powder. The school uniform that said you belonged somewhere. The accent that reveals where you grew up. The values you carry that declare your family. Every identity is a sindoor — a mark of connection applied so visibly that the world reads it before hearing your name.

Name the marks. The ones you wear with pride and the ones you wish you could wash off.
