Wafā

the loyalty

Wafaa is loyalty — not the easy kind that costs nothing, but the kind that endures after the reasons for staying have expired. It is the promise kept when breaking it would have been cheaper, simpler, and entirely forgivable. Wafaa is faithfulness tested by time, by distance, by the slow erosion of everything that once made the bond convenient. Wafā asks you to name the loyalties that defined you — the bonds you honoured past the point of reason and the ones that were honoured for you.

Wafā

Loyalty is simple when it is mutual and the weather is fair. Wafaa begins where simplicity ends — it is the loyalty that persists through betrayal, through silence, through years of unreturned calls. It is the mother who defends a child the world has given up on. It is the friend who shows up at the hospital when they were told not to come. Wafaa does not keep score. That is both its glory and its danger.

Not all wafaa is wise. Sometimes loyalty becomes a prison of its own — a refusal to abandon what has already abandoned you. The line between devotion and self-destruction is drawn by the person doing the staying, and they rarely draw it in time. But when wafaa is true and returned, it becomes the rarest thing in human life: a bond that time cannot degrade.
