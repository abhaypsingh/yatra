Dāg

A stain is not the same as a wound. A wound heals. A stain stays — not as pain, but as a permanent mark on who you are.

The time you were unkind and saw the hurt in someone’s eyes. The exam you cheated on. The friend you abandoned when they needed you. These stains do not make you bad. They make you human and aware.

The cloth is not ruined because it is stained. Some of the most beautiful fabrics in the world are dyed unevenly, imperfectly. Name your stains. They are proof that you lived, stumbled, and kept going.
