Jhālar

the fringe

A jhalar is the decorative fringe that hangs from the edge of things — a curtain, a toran, a shawl. It is ornamental. It is not the thing itself but the edge of the thing. Jhalar asks you to name the fringes — the decorations in your life that made the ordinary beautiful, and the ornaments that hid what was underneath.

Jhālar

We love jhalars. The toran on the door at Diwali. The lace on a grandmother’s tablecloth. The colourful border on a saree. These small decorations turn the mundane into the festive.

But jhalars also hide edges. The pleasant small talk that avoids the real conversation. The family photo that hides the argument that happened five minutes before. The perfect social media post that fringes a messy, beautiful, complicated life.

Name the fringes. Appreciate the ones that beautify. Question the ones that conceal.
