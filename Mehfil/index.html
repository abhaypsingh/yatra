Mehfil

A mehfil cannot be scheduled the way a meeting can. You can arrange the room, invite the people, pour the chai — but the mehfil itself either descends or it does not. It is the moment when conversation stops being an exchange of information and becomes an exchange of truth. When the singer’s voice cracks and the audience feels it in their own throat. When someone tells a story and the room forgets time.

You have attended gatherings that were merely gatherings, and you have attended a few that were mehfils. The difference is unmistakable. In a mehfil, you leave lighter than you arrived. Something was shared that could not have been shared in any other configuration of people, in any other moment.

Remember the mehfils. They are proof that presence — real, unhurried, unguarded presence — is the rarest and most valuable thing humans offer each other.
