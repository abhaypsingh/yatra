where feelings live

Emotions are not just thoughts. They are physical. They live in your body.

Anger tightens your jaw. Sadness sits heavy in your chest. Fear knots your stomach. Joy opens your hands. Most people never notice where their feelings actually are.

Tap the body where you feel something today. Name it. Over time, you will begin to know yourself not just in your mind but in your bones.

The body keeps the score. Learn to read it.

Tap a region of the body. Then choose the feeling that lives there today.

Sparsha स्पर्श

Sparsha (Sanskrit: touch, sensation, contact) is the act of feeling through the body. In yogic tradition, the body is not separate from the mind — it is the mind made visible. Every emotion has a physical address.

This practice is rooted in somatic awareness — the idea that by noticing where feelings live in your body, you gain power over them. Not to suppress, but to understand. The tightness in your chest has a name. The knot in your stomach has a story.
