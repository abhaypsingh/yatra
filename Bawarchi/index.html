A bawarchi does not just cook food. A bawarchi transforms raw things into nourishment through knowledge, patience, and care. Your mother, your grandmother, the street vendor — all bawarchis. Bawarchi asks you to name the cooks — the people who fed you with more than food, who nourished you in ways a plate cannot hold.

Bāwarchī

The bawarchi is the most undervalued artist. Three times a day, they create something from nothing. They know how much salt a family needs — not by measurement but by love. They adjust the spice for the child and the elder differently from the same pot.

But bawarchis are not only in kitchens. The friend who always knows what to say when you are low. The mentor who gives you exactly the challenge you need. The stranger who offered kindness when you were starving for it. All bawarchis. All feeding you.

Name the cooks. They made you who you are, one meal at a time.
